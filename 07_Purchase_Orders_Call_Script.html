How Purchase Orders Fit Into Your Workflow

A Purchase Order in Rev.io PSA is a formal request to buy parts from a vendor — created directly from a ticket and linked to it. This keeps your procurement process tied to the work it's for. When you need to order parts for a job, you create the PO on that ticket. When the parts arrive, you receive them against the PO. That receiving step is what updates your inventory count — so the two workflows are directly connected.

→ Connects ToPurchase Orders reference Providers (vendors) and catalog items (Parts). Receiving a PO increments inventory quantities in the Inventory module. The PO stays visible on the ticket it was created from, so anyone looking at that ticket can see what was ordered, when it was expected, and whether it's been received.
2
Creating a Purchase Order

Open a ticket > Purchase Orders tab > Add Purchase Order. Key fields:

  • Provider — the vendor you're ordering from. These are set up in the Providers module, which we'll cover next.
  • PO Number — assign manually or let the system auto-generate.
  • Line Items — the parts you're ordering. Pull from the product catalog.
  • Quantity and Unit Cost — how many and what you're paying.
  • Expected Delivery Date — optional, but useful for tracking when parts should arrive.
  • Notes — any special instructions for the vendor or your team.
❓ PromptDo you regularly order parts for jobs? Let's create a test PO on an existing ticket now.
3
Receiving a Purchase Order

When parts arrive, open the PO and click Receive. Enter the quantities received — full order or partial. This increments your on-hand inventory count and updates the PO status. The ticket retains the full PO history.

▲ Platform ImpactReceiving is a required step for accurate inventory tracking. If your team receives parts physically but doesn't receive them in PSA, your stock counts will drift and your inventory reports will be unreliable. Build receiving into your process from day one.

Action Items

  • Set up all your regular vendors as Providers (next topic) before creating real POs
  • Try creating a PO on a real ticket
  • Complete the receive step when parts physically arrive
