1
Inventory Management — The Full Picture

Inventory Management in Rev.io PSA tracks physical stock quantities across your locations. It connects to almost everything we've set up today: catalog items (Parts) are what gets tracked, Providers are who you order from, Purchase Orders are how stock comes in, and Tickets are how stock goes out.

It's worth knowing that inventory tracking is optional per item — not every catalog item needs it. Labor rates and flat-fee services definitely don't. Parts you physically stock and replenish do. You control this item by item in the catalog.

❓ PromptDo you keep physical stock — parts on shelves, in vans, in a warehouse? If yes, we'll make sure tracking is enabled for the right items.
→ Connects ToInventory connects to: the Product Catalog (which items are tracked), Purchase Orders (receiving increments stock), Tickets (adding a part to a ticket decrements stock), Warehouses (where stock lives), and Assets (customer-owned equipment you've installed and service).
2
Warehouses & Stock Levels

A warehouse is any physical location where you store parts — your office, a storage unit, or a technician's van. Each location is its own warehouse in PSA. Go to Inventory > Warehouses > Add Warehouse.

❓ PromptWhere do you keep inventory? Do your techs carry their own stock in their vehicles?

Once warehouses are created, you assign stock quantities to each catalog item per warehouse from the item's Inventory tab. You can also set a Reorder Point — a minimum quantity that flags a low-stock alert before you run out. PSA tracks stock movement automatically: parts added to tickets reduce your count, parts received on POs increase it.

▲ Platform ImpactWithout warehouses and opening quantities set up, your inventory counts will be inaccurate from day one. Starting with correct on-hand quantities is important — it's much harder to reconcile inventory after the fact than to enter it correctly at setup.
3
Assets

Assets are a related but separate concept. Where inventory tracks stock quantities in your possession, an Asset represents a specific piece of equipment installed at a customer's location — something you service and maintain over time. When you sell and install a product, you can convert it to an Asset on the customer's account.

Assets live on the customer profile under the Service tab. Each time you create a ticket for that equipment, you link it to the Asset — building a complete service history for that specific piece of hardware over its lifetime.

❓ PromptDo you need to track equipment you've installed at customer sites — things like routers, cameras, alarm panels, or servers?
▲ Platform ImpactAssets are especially valuable for recurring maintenance businesses. Without them, there's no way to see the full history of a specific piece of equipment — every ticket is isolated, and trends like repeated failures or aging equipment become invisible.

Action Items

  • Enable inventory tracking on catalog items that are physical parts
  • Create warehouse locations — one per stocking location or technician vehicle
  • Set opening stock quantities on your most common parts
  • Set reorder points on high-turnover items
  • Start creating assets when equipment is installed at customer sites
