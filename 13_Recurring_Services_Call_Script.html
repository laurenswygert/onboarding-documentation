1
Automating Your Predictable Revenue

Recurring Services automate the charges that happen on a regular schedule — monthly managed service fees, quarterly maintenance plan charges, annual software subscriptions. Instead of manually creating a charge or invoice every month for the same service, you set it up once and PSA generates it automatically on the date you specify.

This is one of the highest-value automations in the platform for businesses with recurring revenue. The time savings compound every month, and it eliminates the human error that comes from manual recurring billing.

→ Connects ToRecurring Services generate Unposted charges in the Billing module on their scheduled billing dates. Those charges are reviewed and posted, then rolled into invoices through the Invoicing workflow. Recurring Services can optionally be linked to a Contract, which ties the charge to the specific agreement it's part of. They reference catalog items for the line item description and pricing.
▲ Platform ImpactIf recurring services aren't set up before go-live, your team will need to manually create charges every billing cycle. This is error-prone and time-consuming — and missed charges are lost revenue that's very hard to recover after the fact.
2
Setting Up a Recurring Service

Go to Customer > Billing tab > Recurring Services > Add Recurring Service. The key fields:

  • Service Item — the catalog item this charge is based on. Controls the description on the invoice.
  • Quantity and Unit Price — how many units at what rate. The total calculates automatically.
  • Billing Frequency — Monthly, Quarterly, Semi-Annual, Annual.
  • Start Date — when billing begins.
  • Next Bill Date — calculated automatically based on start date and frequency. Review this to confirm the first charge will generate when expected.
  • In Advance or In Arrears — does the charge generate at the start of the period (advance) or at the end (arrears)?
  • Contract — optionally link to an active contract for history and reporting.
❓ PromptWhat recurring charges do you bill manually today that we should automate? Let's set one up together.
💡 Internal NoteAfter saving, point out the Next Bill Date explicitly. Clients often want to know exactly when the first charge will appear. If the start date is mid-month, clarify whether the first charge will be pro-rated or full.
3
How Charges Flow to Billing

When the Next Bill Date arrives, PSA automatically creates an Unposted charge on the customer's billing account. You'll find it under the customer's Billing tab > Unposted sub-tab. From there you review, confirm it's correct, and post it. Posting moves it to the Ledger and makes it available for invoicing.

This review step is intentional — it keeps you in control of what goes out while PSA handles the generation automatically.

Action Items

  • Identify every customer who has a recurring charge you're currently handling manually
  • Set up a recurring service for each one
  • Verify Next Bill Dates align with your billing schedule
  • Decide on in advance vs. in arrears and apply consistently
