1
The Dispatch Board — Command Center for Field Operations

The Dispatch Board is a purpose-built scheduling interface for managing field teams. It's different from the Calendar view you may have seen — while the Calendar shows a single user's schedule, the Dispatch Board shows your entire team side by side, with unscheduled tickets queued up and ready to assign. It's designed for a dispatcher to manage multiple technicians at once in real time.

→ Connects ToThe Dispatch Board pulls from: Users and User Groups (who appears on the board), open Tickets (the unscheduled work queue), Business Hours configured in System Settings (availability windows), and Customer Addresses (for location context). When a ticket is dispatched, a calendar event is created on the technician's schedule. The tech sees this in the Rev.io mobile app and can update status, log time, and add notes from the field.
2
The Board Layout & Dispatching

The grid shows technicians across one axis and time across the other. Your unscheduled tickets appear in a separate list panel — these are open tickets that haven't been assigned to a time slot yet. To dispatch: drag a ticket from the unscheduled list and drop it onto a technician's time slot. A dialog appears to confirm the start time, estimated duration, and any notes for the tech. Confirm and the event is created.

❓ PromptLet's practice right now — grab one of the open tickets and assign it to a technician. Pick a date and time and let's see how it looks.
💡 Internal NoteAfter they dispatch, show them how the ticket moves from the unscheduled list to the schedule grid. Then ask them to check the assigned tech's calendar view to see it reflected there too.
3
Availability, Filters & Field Communication

The board respects your configured business hours and shows existing events — so if a tech is already booked during a time slot, the board makes that visible and helps you avoid double-booking. If you have a large team, use the User Group filter to focus the board on just the relevant team.

❓ PromptDo any of your technicians have non-standard schedules or days they're regularly unavailable? Those can be configured on their user record so the board reflects their actual availability.

Once a ticket is dispatched, the technician sees their schedule in the Rev.io mobile app. From their phone, they can: accept the dispatch, update ticket status, log time, add activity notes, capture a customer signature, and mark the job complete. Everything they do in the app syncs back to PSA in real time, so dispatchers see the latest status without having to call or text the tech.

▲ Platform ImpactThe Dispatch Board and mobile app together are what eliminate the need for a separate scheduling tool. Without them, dispatch happens via phone, text, or a whiteboard — all of which create gaps in visibility and make it impossible to report on technician utilization.

Action Items

  • Practice dispatching 5–10 open tickets on the board
  • Set user availability for any techs with non-standard schedules
  • Download the Rev.io mobile app and have at least one tech set it up before going live
