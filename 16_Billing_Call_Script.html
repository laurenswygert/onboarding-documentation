1
How Everything Flows Into Billing

Billing is where all the work your team has done becomes revenue. Every ticket your techs close, every recurring service that fires, every contract fee that generates — it all flows into the Billing module and sits as an Unposted charge, waiting to be reviewed and turned into an invoice.

Think of billing as the final step in a chain that started with your product catalog. You built the catalog so charges would be consistent. Your techs added catalog items to tickets. The ticket got closed. Now those charges are here, ready to go to a customer.

→ Connects ToBilling receives charges from: Tickets (Parts & Labor and Time Logs when a ticket is sent to billing), Recurring Services (auto-generated on their billing dates), Contract Billing Parameters (flat recurring fees), and Manual Charges (added directly from a customer's Quick Actions). From Billing, charges flow to Invoices which are sent to customers. Payments then come back in and are applied to those invoices on the customer's Ledger.
▲ Platform ImpactThe Billing module is the final checkpoint before money leaves or arrives. If charges are incorrect here — wrong quantities, wrong catalog items, missing charges — that error hits the customer. A clean, reviewed billing process protects your revenue and your customer relationships.
2
The Unposted Tab — Your Review Checkpoint

Every charge that enters billing starts as Unposted. Go to a customer account > Billing tab > Unposted sub-tab. You'll see all charges that have been generated but not yet finalized. This is your opportunity to review before anything goes to the customer.

From Unposted you can: verify quantities and prices, edit a charge if something is wrong, add charges that were missed, and remove anything that shouldn't be there. When everything looks correct, you post the charges — which moves them to the Ledger and makes them available for invoicing.

❓ PromptDo you want to review every charge individually before posting, or would you prefer to set up rules that auto-post certain types of charges?
💡 Internal NoteMost clients start by reviewing manually. It builds confidence in the process. As they get comfortable, many switch to automated posting for standard recurring charges while still reviewing ticket-based charges manually.
3
The Ledger

The Ledger is the complete financial history of a customer account — every charge, every payment, every credit, every invoice, in chronological order. It's your first stop when a customer calls with a billing question.

From the Ledger you can see the current balance, what's been billed, what's been paid, and what's still outstanding. Everything is traceable back to its source — a specific ticket, a recurring service, a contract fee.

4
Recording Payments

When a customer pays, you record it under the customer's Billing tab > Payment History > Add Payment. Select the amount, the payment method, and which invoice(s) to apply it to. The invoice status updates to Paid and the Ledger reflects the payment. If you have a payment processor connected, payments made through the portal or via autopay record automatically — no manual entry needed.

❓ PromptHow do your customers pay today — check, card, ACH? And do you have any customers who you want on autopay?
→ Connects ToPayment recording works with: Payment Processors (if connected, electronic payments are automatic), the customer Ledger (payments reduce the outstanding balance), and Invoice status (paid invoices are marked and closed). We'll configure Payment Processors in the next session.

Action Items

  • Close a test ticket and trace the charge through to Unposted
  • Post the charge and review it on the Ledger
  • Decide your billing cycle — how often will you invoice?
  • Identify which customers you want on autopay before the Payment Processors session
