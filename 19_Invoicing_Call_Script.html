1
Invoicing — Turning Charges Into Revenue

Invoicing is the final step in your billing cycle. You've done the work, the charges are posted on the Ledger, the template is configured, and the payment processor is connected. Now you take those posted charges, group them into an invoice, and send it to the customer. This is where the work your team has done officially becomes revenue.

→ Connects ToInvoicing takes posted charges from the Ledger, formats them using your Invoice Template, delivers them to the customer contact's email address, and — if the customer portal or autopay is enabled — triggers the payment collection workflow. Payments applied to the invoice update its status on the Ledger and close the loop on the billing cycle.
2
Creating an Invoice

Individual invoice: Customer > Billing tab > Invoices sub-tab > Create Invoice. PSA pulls all posted, uninvoiced charges for that customer into a draft automatically.

Batch invoicing: From the Billing module main navigation, filter customers with uninvoiced charges and generate invoices for multiple accounts in one step. This is the most efficient approach when you invoice on a regular monthly cycle.

❓ PromptHow often do you plan to invoice — monthly batch, on ticket close, or on demand? Let's run through a real invoice together right now.
3
Review, Send & Collect

Review: Every invoice starts in Draft status. While in Draft, you can verify all line items, adjust dates, add a memo, and make corrections. Once posted, changes require a Credit Memo or Rebill — so review carefully before posting.

Send: Post the invoice and send it. PSA formats it using your template and emails it to the customer's billing contact with a link to view, download, and pay online.

Credits and Rebilling: If a correction is needed after posting — issue a Credit Memo to offset a charge on the customer's account, or use the Rebill function to correct ticket charges and re-invoice. These keep your records clean while still allowing for corrections.

▲ Platform ImpactThe most common billing error is posting and sending before reviewing. A charge error that reaches the customer requires a credit memo, a conversation, and sometimes a rebuilt relationship. A review step before sending catches most problems before they become issues.

Action Items

  • Run your first real invoice for a customer with posted charges
  • Send a test invoice to yourself to verify the template and email look correct
  • Set your recurring invoicing schedule — weekly, monthly, on ticket close?
  • Confirm autopay is configured for eligible customers so payments collect automatically
