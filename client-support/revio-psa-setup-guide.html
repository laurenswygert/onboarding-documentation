User Management

1 / 19Step 1 of 19
💡What This Is & Why It Matters

User Management is the foundation of your PSA. Every person on your team who needs to access the system must have an account here — technicians, dispatchers, account managers, billing staff, and administrators alike.

Getting this right early matters because user accounts control what your team can see and do. Permissions and roles determine whether a technician can close a ticket, whether a manager can approve quotes, and whether someone can access billing data. Starting with clean user setup prevents confusion and security headaches later.

How it connects to the rest of the platform:

Ticketing (assigned techs) Scheduling (dispatch groups) Billing (approvals) Quoting (ownership)
🔑Key Concepts

Roles define a user's job function (e.g., Technician, Account Manager, Admin). Permissions control exactly what they can see and do within each role. User Groups let you cluster users by department or team — this is especially important for ticket routing, where incoming tickets can be automatically assigned to a group.

💡
Good to know: During initial setup and testing, consider making your first users Global Admins so they can access everything. You can refine permissions once you understand how your team uses the system day-to-day.
✅What to Set Up
  • ✓
    Navigate to Admin → User Management and add your first user
  • ✓
    Set the user's name, email address, and login username
  • ✓
    Assign a role (start with Global Admin for key staff during testing)
  • ✓
    Add remaining team members who will use the system
  • ✓
    Create at least one User Group that matches a team or department
  • ✓
    Verify each new user receives and completes their welcome/password email (valid for 12 hours)

Custom permission sets, role-based access control, and Single Sign-On (SSO) integrations require configuration assistance from our team. If you're unsure how to structure permissions for complex org hierarchies, we can help you design it.

System Settings

2 / 19Step 2 of 19
💡What This Is & Why It Matters

System Settings are the control center for how your PSA behaves. These settings affect every part of the platform — from how tickets are created, to how your team logs time, to what customers see when they interact with you. Most of these are decisions you only make once, so it's worth thinking them through carefully.

How it connects:

Ticketing (defaults & routing) Scheduling (business hours) Billing (signature clause) Customer portal
🏢Company Information

This is what appears on invoices, customer communications, and your customer-facing portal. Make sure it's accurate before you go live.

  • ✓
    Verify your company name and address are correct under Admin → System Settings → Company
  • ✓
    Set your business hours (these are used for SLA calculations and scheduling)
  • ✓
    Add your company holidays (so SLA timers pause and scheduling reflects your actual calendar)
📱SMS Notifications

Rev.io PSA can send text message notifications internally to your team and outbound to your customers. Decide whether you want these enabled and how customers are opted in.

Decision: Internal SMS Notifications

Should your team receive SMS alerts for ticket assignments, updates, or escalations?

Decision: Customer SMS Notifications

Should customers receive texts about their ticket status or appointments? Do you want customers opted in automatically, or should they opt in themselves?

🎫Ticket Defaults

These settings control how every new ticket enters your system. Getting these right means less manual cleanup and better data consistency from day one.

  • ✓
    New Ticket Status: What status should tickets have when they first come in? (e.g., "New", "Open", "Pending")
  • ✓
    Default Ticket Type: What type do you assume a ticket is before triage? (e.g., "Break/Fix", "Support Request")
  • ✓
    Default Priority: Where should new tickets start in your priority queue?
  • ✓
    Default User Group: Which team or group should receive new tickets initially for triage?
  • ✓
    Email Public Activity: Should customers automatically receive an email when a public note is added to their ticket?
  • ✓
    Auto-assign by Email Domain: Should tickets from a known email domain automatically link to the matching customer account?
⚙️Additional Settings
  • ✓
    Signature Acceptance Clause: Review the default legal verbiage for your work orders and service agreements. Customize it to match your business.
  • ✓
    Ticket Tags: Decide whether your team should be able to add tags to tickets for categorization and filtering.
  • ✓
    Time Clock Reasons: Define the reasons your technicians can select when clocking in or out (e.g., "On Site", "Remote Work", "Break").
💡
System Tables: Under Admin → System Settings → System Tables you'll find all your ticket types, statuses, severities, and priorities. This is a great area to customize before your first real tickets come in.

Some system settings — particularly SLA configurations, customer portal branding, and advanced notification rules — are best reviewed with our team to make sure they align with your service delivery model.

Product Catalog

4 / 19Step 4 of 19
💡What This Is & Why It Matters

Your Product Catalog is the master list of everything you sell — labor, hardware, software licenses, managed services, and any other billable items. This catalog feeds directly into quoting, ticketing, recurring services, and invoicing. If a product isn't in the catalog, it can't be billed.

Setting up your catalog well now means faster quoting, consistent pricing, and accurate invoices. Every product you add here defines its price, how it's taxed, and how it appears on customer-facing documents.

Quoting Ticketing (billable items) Recurring Services Invoicing Inventory
📦Product Types to Add

Think through every type of thing your company charges for. Common categories include:

  • Labor / Service: Hourly rates for your technicians by skill level or role
  • Hardware: Physical products you sell or install (equipment, parts, devices)
  • Software / Licenses: Software you resell or manage on behalf of customers
  • Managed Services: Flat-rate recurring services (e.g., monthly monitoring)
  • Expenses: Reimbursable costs like travel or third-party fees
✅What to Set Up
  • ✓
    Navigate to Admin → Product Catalog and add your first product
  • ✓
    Set the product name, description, SKU (if applicable), and unit price
  • ✓
    Assign the correct product category and type
  • ✓
    Indicate whether the product is taxable
  • ✓
    Set the cost (your actual cost) if you want to track margins
  • ✓
    Add your core labor rate(s) as products (e.g., "Standard Labor – Hourly")
  • ✓
    Continue adding your most commonly used products and services
💡
Start with your top 20: You don't need to load your entire catalog before going live. Start with your most frequently sold items and add more over time.

Billing

14 / 19Step 14 of 19
💡What This Is & Why It Matters

The Billing module is how Rev.io PSA turns your service delivery into revenue. Time entries, parts, and services logged on tickets get collected here and converted into invoices. Getting billing set up correctly is critical — it's the final step that ensures your team's work actually gets paid.

Billing in PSA is flexible: you can bill per ticket, in batches, on a schedule, or as part of a recurring service. Understanding the flow before you go live prevents invoice errors and billing disputes.

Ticketing Bill Profiles Invoice Templates Payment Processors Recurring Services
🔄How the Billing Flow Works

Understanding this flow will help everything in Phase 3 click into place:

  1. Technicians log time and parts on a ticket
  2. A manager reviews the ticket and marks it ready to bill
  3. The ticket's billable items are sent to the billing queue
  4. An invoice is generated from those items using your Invoice Template
  5. The invoice is sent to the customer and payment is collected
⚠️
Reminder: Do not click "Send to Billing" on tickets during testing — that creates real invoices. Use clearly labeled test customers and data.
Rev.io Assistance RequiredInitial billing configuration requires our team

Billing setup — including connecting your accounting system, configuring billing cycles, setting up batch billing runs, and enabling the billing queue — requires a configuration session with our team. This ensures your billing is accurate and your financial data flows correctly from day one.
