1
What System Settings Controls

System Settings is the configuration layer that governs how the entire Rev.io PSA platform behaves. Every setting here has downstream effects — on tickets, on SLAs, on billing, on scheduling, on notifications, and on the documents that go out to your customers. Getting System Settings right during initial setup prevents configuration problems from rippling through every other module.

→ Connects ToSystem Settings is referenced by: the Service module (ticket defaults, statuses, types, priorities), the Scheduling module (business hours for availability), SLA calculations (business hours and holidays), the Billing module (company info on invoices), SMS notifications (team and customer), and every ticket created in the system (defaults applied automatically).
2
Company Settings — Full Coverage

Navigate to Admin > System Settings > Company.

Company Name & Address

The company name and address configured here appear on every customer-facing document generated by PSA: invoices, quotes, work orders, proposals, and signature forms. This should match your legal business name and the address you want on official documents — which may differ from your physical office location.

❓ PromptPlease confirm your company's legal name and the address you want on customer-facing documents. Is this your billing address, your registered address, or your primary office?
⚠ Watch OutIf your company name on documents doesn't match your legal name, it can create issues with customer payments and accounting reconciliation. Confirm this with your finance team before going live.

Business Hours

Business Hours define when your company is officially operating. This setting has two critical downstream effects: it controls the SLA timer (time outside business hours doesn't count toward SLA response time) and it gates availability on the Dispatch Board (techs aren't shown as available outside configured hours).

You can configure different hours for different days — for example, 8 AM to 6 PM Monday through Friday, 9 AM to 1 PM Saturday, and closed Sunday. You can also configure multiple shifts if needed.

❓ PromptWhat are your standard business hours by day of week? Do you have any days with different hours, or any days you're closed?
💡 Pro TipIf you have techs on call after hours, don't extend standard business hours to cover it — that would affect SLA timers for all tickets. Instead, configure on-call through user availability settings and handle after-hours separately.

Company Holidays

Company Holidays are specific calendar dates that are excluded from SLA calculations entirely. If a ticket comes in on December 24th and Christmas Eve is a holiday, the SLA clock doesn't run. If it's not listed as a holiday, SLA time accrues as a normal business day.

Add holidays for the full calendar year. You'll need to update this list annually.

❓ PromptDo you have your company holiday schedule for the year? Let's add them all now. If not, we'll make it a follow-up action item — but note that any time between now and when you add them will count as business time for SLA purposes.

SMS Notifications

Rev.io PSA supports SMS notifications in two separate directions: outbound to your internal team, and outbound to your customers. These are independently configured and each has an opt-in model decision.

SettingWhat It ControlsCommon Choice
Internal SMSWhether team members receive text alerts for ticket assignments, status changes, and other system eventsOn for mobile field teams; often off for office-only staff
Customer SMSWhether customers receive text updates when their ticket is created, updated, assigned, or resolvedOn for B2C service businesses; sometimes off for enterprise B2B
Customer Auto Opt-InWhether customers are automatically opted in to SMS upon account creation, or must actively opt inAuto opt-in is simpler operationally; manual opt-in may be required for compliance
❓ PromptDo you want SMS enabled for your internal team? For customers? If yes on customers, should they be auto-opted in or manually opt in?
⚠ Watch OutIf you operate in a regulated industry or have customers in jurisdictions with strict SMS marketing laws (like TCPA in the US), verify your legal requirements before enabling customer auto opt-in. Non-compliance can result in significant fines.
4
Services — Ticket Configuration

This section controls the default behavior of every ticket created in the system — before anyone touches it. These settings create consistency across your team and ensure tickets always start in a known, expected state.

New Ticket Status

The status assigned to a ticket the moment it is created — whether by a tech, via email-to-ticket, or through the customer portal. This should represent the very beginning of your ticket lifecycle, before any triage has happened.

❓ PromptWhen a brand new ticket comes in, what status should it start at? Common answers: 'New,' 'Open,' 'Submitted.' What represents 'just arrived, not yet looked at' in your workflow?

Assumed Ticket Type

The ticket type the system assumes before a technician has triaged and classified the ticket. This is a placeholder — it ensures no ticket is left without a type, while signaling that classification hasn't happened yet. Common values: 'General,' 'Unclassified,' 'Unknown.'

Default Ticket Type

The ticket type that's pre-selected when a technician manually creates a new ticket. This should be the most common type of work your team creates tickets for. Saves techs from having to select the type on every ticket they create.

❓ PromptWhat type of work makes up the majority of your ticket volume? That should be your default.

Ticket Priority Levels

The priority options available when creating or editing a ticket, and the default priority assigned to new tickets. Your priority scale should match how your team actually talks about urgency.

Common ScaleLevelsGood For
3-LevelLow, Medium, HighSimple operations with clear escalation paths
4-LevelLow, Medium, High, CriticalMSPs and IT support with defined escalation tiers
NumberedP1, P2, P3, P4Enterprise IT environments following ITIL frameworks
❓ PromptWhat priority levels do you use today? And when a ticket first comes in before triage, what priority should it start at?

Default Ticket User Group

The user group that new tickets are assigned to automatically. This is the first-response team — the queue that catches all inbound tickets before they're routed to a specific technician. This field references the user groups created in User Management.

❓ PromptIs there a default team or queue that all new tickets should land in first? This is typically a general support or intake group.
5
Ticket Behavior Settings

Email Out Public Activity Messages

When enabled, every time a public activity note is added to a ticket, the customer automatically receives an email with that update. This is the primary mechanism for proactive customer communication — your team writes a public note, the customer gets notified without anyone having to remember to send a separate email.

❓ PromptDo you want customers to automatically receive an email when a public note is added to their ticket?
💡 Pro TipHighly recommended to enable. It reduces inbound 'what's the status?' calls significantly. Make sure techs understand the difference between internal notes (team-only) and public notes (customer-facing) so they don't accidentally expose internal information.

Auto-Assign Tickets by Email Domain

When enabled, the system automatically links inbound email tickets to a customer account based on the sender's email domain. If someone from @acmecorp.com emails your support address and you have Acme Corp as a customer with that domain registered, the ticket is automatically associated with that account.

❓ PromptWould you like tickets auto-assigned by email domain? Note: this works best when your customers have unique domains. If many customers use generic providers like gmail.com, this setting may cause incorrect matches.
⚠ Watch OutDo not enable auto-assign by domain if you work with many individual consumers who use generic email providers (Gmail, Yahoo, Outlook). The system could match unrelated customers incorrectly.

Signature Acceptance Clause

The legal text that appears when a customer digitally signs off on completed work through the PSA portal or mobile app. There is a default clause in the system. Review it carefully — this is the language your customers agree to when accepting your work.

❓ PromptLet's review the current default clause together. Is the wording acceptable for your business, or do you need to customize it? If changes are needed, what should it say?
⚠ Watch OutIf you make changes to the signature clause, have your legal team review it before going live. This is a legally binding document.

Ticket Tags

When enabled, users can add freeform text tags to any ticket. Tags are searchable and filterable, allowing teams to label tickets with cross-cutting categories that don't fit into ticket type or status — things like 'VIP Customer,' 'Recurring Issue,' 'Warranty,' or 'Hardware Failure.'

💡 Pro TipTags work best when you establish a standard tag taxonomy with your team. Without structure, tags become inconsistent and lose their filtering value. Create a short list of approved tags and communicate them during team training.

Time Clock Reasons

The categories your team selects when logging time on a ticket. Every time entry in PSA requires a time clock reason — it tells the system (and your reports) not just how much time was spent, but what kind of work was done.

Common reason sets:

  • On-Site Labor, Remote Support, Travel Time, Project Management, Training, Administrative
  • Installation, Troubleshooting, Preventive Maintenance, Emergency Response
  • Billable, Non-Billable, Warranty, Internal
❓ PromptWhat categories do you use to classify time today? Let's build your complete time clock reason list right now. These will appear on every ticket timesheet and feed into billing and payroll reporting.
▲ Platform ImpactTime clock reasons are critical if you bill time to customers. They appear on invoices and determine how hours are bucketed in every time-based report. Vague or inconsistent reasons make billing disputes impossible to resolve and profitability reporting inaccurate.

Action Items

  • Confirm company name and address match your legal business name
  • Configure business hours for every day of the week
  • Add all company holidays for the current year
  • Configure SMS settings — internal and customer-facing
  • Set all ticket defaults: status, assumed type, default type, priority, user group
  • Enable email out public activity if desired
  • Configure auto-assign by domain if appropriate for your customer base
  • Review and customize the signature acceptance clause
  • Enable ticket tags and define a standard tag taxonomy with your team
  • Build the complete list of time clock reasons
