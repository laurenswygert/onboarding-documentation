The Customer Record as the Platform Anchor

In Rev.io PSA, every piece of work, every financial transaction, and every document ties back to a customer record. Tickets require a customer. Invoices are sent to a customer. Quotes are built for a customer. Contracts live under a customer. Assets are installed at a customer. Recurring services bill to a customer.

A complete, well-structured customer database is not just a contact list — it's the operational foundation of the entire platform. Data quality here determines the quality of everything downstream: billing accuracy, ticket routing, customer communication, and reporting.

→ Connects ToCustomer records are the parent record for: Tickets (every ticket requires a linked customer), Billing (invoices, payments, charges, and ledger all belong to a customer), Quotes and Opportunities, Contracts and Recurring Services, Assets, Calendar Events (can be linked to a customer), and the Customer Portal (portal access is managed per customer).

❓ PromptLet's create your first customer together. Use a real account or a test record — walk through every field so you understand what each one does.
💡 Pro TipDon't skip Customer Type and Stage — they seem optional but become very valuable for filtering your customer list and for segment-level reporting in Analytics. It's much harder to retroactively categorize thousands of customers than to set it during creation.

Contacts — Structure and Best Practices

A Contact is an individual person at the customer account. Every customer should have at least one contact — the person who calls in for support, receives invoices, and signs off on work. Most business customers will have multiple contacts with different roles.

From the customer record > Overview tab > Contacts sub-tab > Add Contact.

FieldDescriptionWhy It Matters
First / Last NameContact's full nameAppears on tickets, email communications, and portal access.
EmailContact's email addressUsed for ticket notifications, portal login, invoice delivery, and email-to-ticket domain matching. Most important field on the contact.
Phone / MobileContact phone numbersMobile is used for SMS notifications if enabled.
Role / TitleJob title at their companyHelps your team know who they're dealing with when a ticket comes in.
Primary ContactDesignates the main contact for the accountThe Primary Contact receives all account-level notifications by default. Every customer should have exactly one.
Billing ContactDesignates this person to receive invoicesInvoices are sent to the billing contact's email. Can be different from the primary contact.
Portal AccessWhether this contact can log into the customer portalEnable to give the contact a login. They can view tickets, submit requests, and pay invoices depending on portal configuration.
Do Not ContactSuppresses all outbound communication to this contactUse when a contact has opted out or when communication should be routed elsewhere.
❓ PromptAdd the primary contact and billing contact for the customer we just created. In many cases they'll be the same person — but confirm.
▲ Platform ImpactThe contact's email address is the most important field. It drives email-to-ticket domain matching, ticket notification emails, portal access, and invoice delivery. An incorrect or missing email means the customer won't receive updates about their own service tickets.
4
The Customer Profile — Full Navigation

The customer profile is organized into four main tabs, each representing a different aspect of the customer relationship. Your team will use different tabs depending on their role.

Overview Tab

The primary account information view. Contains: contact list, portal users, related/child accounts, account notes and journal entries, tags, and account history summary. This is where account managers and dispatchers spend most of their time when looking up a customer.

Service Tab

The complete service history for this customer. Sub-tabs include: Tickets (all open and historical tickets), Calendar (all scheduled work for this customer), Assets (all equipment installed at this customer's locations), Contracts (all active and historical service agreements), and Recurring Services (all repeating charges and scheduled services).

Billing Tab

The complete financial picture of the customer. Sub-tabs include: Ledger (every charge, payment, credit, and adjustment in chronological order), Invoices (all invoices — draft, sent, paid, and voided), Unposted Charges (pending charges awaiting review and posting), Payment History (all payments received), Payment Accounts (stored credit card and bank account tokens), and Recurring Services billing view.

Quotes Tab

All quoting activity for this customer. Sub-tabs include: Quotes (all active and historical quotes), Opportunities (pipeline tracking for potential work), and Proposals (all proposals sent and their acceptance status).

5
Related Accounts and Account Hierarchy

Rev.io PSA supports parent-child account relationships. If you have enterprise customers with multiple locations, subsidiaries, or divisions — each with their own billing and service history — you can create each as its own customer account and link them together as related accounts.

From the customer's Overview tab, you can add Related Accounts. The parent account provides a consolidated view while each child account maintains its own independent tickets, billing, and contracts.

❓ PromptDo any of your customers have multiple locations that need to be managed separately but tied to a parent account?
💡 Pro TipRelated accounts are especially valuable for national or multi-location enterprise customers where each site has its own service team and billing contact, but account management needs a consolidated view of the whole relationship.
6
Customer Portal Overview

The Customer Portal gives your customers a self-service interface where they can view their open tickets, submit new service requests, see their invoice history, and pay invoices online. Portal access is managed at the contact level — you grant individual contacts portal access from the customer's Overview tab.

Portal capabilities typically include: submit a new ticket, view open and closed tickets on their account, add notes or files to tickets, view invoice history and pay outstanding invoices, and view their service agreements and assets.

❓ PromptDo you want to enable portal access for your customers? Which customers and which contacts would you like to start with?
→ Connects ToThe customer portal connects to: Ticket creation and viewing, Billing and invoice payment, Payment Processors (portal payments require a connected processor), Contact email (portal login and notifications), and Customer-facing ticket notification settings.

Action Items

  • Add all active customers with complete information (name, type, stage, address)
  • Add at least one Primary Contact and one Billing Contact per customer
  • Confirm contact email addresses are current and accurate
  • Set Business Hours overrides for any customers with unusual operating hours
  • Tag VIP or high-priority accounts for easy filtering
  • Configure portal access for customers who should have self-service capability
  • Set up parent-child relationships for any multi-location enterprise customers
