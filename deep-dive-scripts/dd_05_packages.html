What Packages Are and Why They Matter

Packages bundle multiple catalog items into a single, named offering. When added to a quote or ticket, the package expands into all its component items automatically. This means your team quotes complex offerings in seconds instead of minutes, pricing is always correct and consistent, and nothing gets left off.

→ Connects ToPackages appear in: Quoting (as a single selectable line item that expands into components), Tickets via the Parts & Labor tab, and can be used in Recurring Services if your managed service charge is a packaged bundle. All component catalog items (Parts, Labor, Services) remain individually tracked in billing and reporting.
▲ Platform ImpactWithout packages, your team builds the same complex quote from scratch every time. This creates inconsistency — one tech includes everything, another forgets a line item. Packages standardize your offerings and protect margins.

3
Pricing Strategies for Packages

Flat Package Price vs. Sum of Components

When you set a flat package price, the component items lose their individual prices in the customer's view — the customer sees one line item at one price. When you let the price sum from components, each item is visible with its individual price.

ApproachProsConsBest For
Flat priceSimpler customer invoice, easier to discountLess line-item visibilityConsumer/residential customers, fixed-price contracts
Sum of componentsFull transparency, easier to adjust individual itemsMore complex invoice, harder to bundle discountEnterprise customers, itemized billing requirements

Tiered Package Structures

If you offer tiered service levels (Basic/Standard/Premium or Silver/Gold/Platinum), create one package per tier. Name them clearly and ensure each tier's component items reflect exactly what's included at that level.

💡 Pro TipCreate a 'package comparison' document alongside your PSA setup that shows customers what's in each tier side by side. This lives outside PSA but references your package names — it makes the sales conversation cleaner.
4
Optional Items and Customer Customization

When building a package, you can mark individual items as optional. When a customer receives a proposal containing this package, they see the package with required items pre-selected and optional items they can add or remove.

This is useful for: add-on services that complement the base package, equipment upgrades (standard vs. premium hardware), warranty extensions, or additional coverage tiers.

❓ PromptAre there items in your packages that customers sometimes add and sometimes don't? Those should be flagged as optional.

Action Items

  • Identify all standard bundled offerings your team sells regularly
  • Build a package for each bundled offering
  • Build tiered packages if you have service tiers
  • Define which items are required vs. optional in each package
  • Set package-level pricing or verify component sum is correct
  • Test each package by adding it to a draft quote to verify it expands correctly
