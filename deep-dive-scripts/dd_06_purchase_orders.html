How POs Fit Into the Service Workflow

Purchase Orders in Rev.io PSA are created directly from a ticket and linked to it permanently. When your team needs parts for a job, they create the PO on the ticket, order from the vendor, receive the parts when they arrive, and the inventory count updates automatically. The ticket becomes the single record of everything related to that job — the work, the communication, the parts ordered, and the time spent.

→ Connects ToPurchase Orders connect: Tickets (POs are created from and linked to a ticket), Providers (every PO must select a vendor), the Product Catalog (Parts referenced on POs), Inventory (receiving a PO increments warehouse stock), and Billing (parts received can be added to the ticket's Parts & Labor tab and billed to the customer).

Creating a Purchase Order — All Fields

Open a ticket > Purchase Orders tab > Add Purchase Order.

FieldDescriptionNotes
ProviderThe vendor you're ordering fromMust be set up in the Providers module. Required.

Expected Delivery DateWhen parts are expected to arriveOptional but useful for job scheduling — dispatchers can see when parts will be ready.

3
Receiving a Purchase Order

When parts arrive, open the PO from the ticket and click Receive. You can receive the full order at once or receive partial quantities if the shipment is split.

Receiving Options

  • Full receipt: All items in the correct quantities. Click 'Receive All' and save.
  • Partial receipt: Some items or fewer quantities than ordered. Enter the quantity received for each item. The PO status shows as 'Partially Received.' Receive the remainder when it arrives.
  • Over-receipt: More items than ordered (vendor shipped extra). You can accept the overage — it increments inventory by the actual received quantity.
  • Returns: If items need to be returned, note it in the PO comments and adjust inventory manually from the warehouse view.
⚠ Watch OutReceiving is mandatory for accurate inventory tracking. If your team receives parts physically but doesn't receive them in PSA, your stock counts will drift — eventually making reorder decisions unreliable. Build receiving into your physical parts arrival process from day one.
💡 Pro TipPost a reminder at your receiving area (literal physical sign) that says 'Received a package? Mark it received in PSA.' The disconnect between physical and digital receiving is the most common inventory accuracy problem.

5
Billing Parts From a PO

Once parts are received, they can be added to the ticket's Parts & Labor tab and billed to the customer. Receiving a PO does not automatically add charges to the ticket — this is intentional, as not all purchased parts may be billable (some may be replacements under warranty or internal use).

The workflow is: create PO → receive parts → add billable parts to the Parts & Labor tab → close ticket → bill customer.

❓ PromptWhen you order parts for a job, do you typically pass the cost through to the customer, or do you absorb it into a flat-fee service charge?

Action Items

  • Set up all vendors as Providers before creating real POs
  • Train team on creating POs from tickets — not as standalone orders
  • Establish a physical receiving process that includes marking POs received in PSA
  • Decide your billing policy for ordered parts and document it for your team
  • Set expected delivery dates on all POs for job scheduling visibility
