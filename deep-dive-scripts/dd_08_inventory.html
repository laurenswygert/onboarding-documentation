The Full Inventory Picture

Inventory Management in Rev.io PSA connects every part of your procurement and service delivery workflow. Parts in the catalog define what you track. Warehouses define where you stock it. Purchase Orders define how stock comes in. Tickets define how stock goes out. Assets define where installed equipment lives at customer sites. Everything connects.

→ Connects ToInventory connects to: Product Catalog (Parts with tracking enabled), Warehouses (where stock is held), Purchase Orders (receiving increments stock), Tickets Parts & Labor (adding a tracked part to a ticket decrements stock), Assets (installed customer equipment), Providers (who you order from), and Analytics (inventory valuation and usage reports).
▲ Platform ImpactInventory tracking only delivers value if it's maintained consistently. The two most common failure points are: (1) not receiving POs in PSA when parts physically arrive, and (2) not adding parts to tickets when they're used on a job. Both cause inventory counts to drift from reality. Establish these habits before go-live.
2
Enabling Inventory Management

Inventory Management is enabled under Admin > System Settings. Once enabled, catalog items that have 'Track Inventory' toggled on will have their quantities tracked. Items without inventory tracking are unaffected — Labor rates and Services should never have tracking enabled.

Per-item inventory tracking is set on each catalog item's record under the Inventory tab. This is where you also set the warehouse and opening quantity.

❓ PromptConfirm that inventory tracking is enabled in your System Settings. Then let's go through your catalog and make sure tracking is only enabled on physical Parts, not on Labor or Services.
⚠ Watch OutEnabling inventory tracking on a Labor item (like 'On-Site Labor') makes no sense — it would attempt to track quantity of hours as physical stock. Audit your catalog after enabling inventory to ensure only physical Parts have tracking on.

Warehouses — Configuration and Structure

Navigate to Inventory > Warehouses > Add Warehouse. A warehouse is any physical location where you store parts.

FieldDescriptionExample
NameDescriptive location nameMain Office, East Warehouse, John's Van, Remote Depot
AddressPhysical location addressUsed for location reference and potential shipping integration
ActiveWhether this location is currently in useDeactivate seasonal or closed locations

Common Warehouse Structures

Business TypeRecommended Warehouse Setup
Single officeOne main warehouse
Multiple officesOne warehouse per office location
Field service with vansOne main warehouse + one per vehicle ('John Smith — Van')
Drop-ship modelMain warehouse + customer site locations as needed
Multi-branch operationOne warehouse per branch, plus one main distribution hub
💡 Pro TipName van/vehicle warehouses with the technician's name: 'Sarah Johnson — Van'. This makes it immediately clear whose inventory is whose on the dispatch board and in reports.
4
Stock Levels, Reorder Points, and Opening Quantities

From each catalog item, navigate to the Inventory tab to set quantities per warehouse.

SettingDescriptionNotes
Quantity on HandCurrent stock at this warehouseSet this accurately at setup. It's your baseline — everything is calculated from here.
Reorder PointMinimum quantity before low-stock alert firesSet to the quantity at which you need to order to avoid running out before the next delivery arrives.
Reorder QuantityHow many to order when reorder point is hitOptional. Documents your standard order quantity.
Preferred VendorDefault vendor for this item at this locationLinks to a Provider. Pre-populates on POs created for this item.

Opening quantities require a physical count. Schedule a stock count before go-live and enter the results directly into PSA. This is the most important data entry step in inventory setup.

⚠ Watch OutDo not enter estimated opening quantities. A wrong starting count means every future report, reorder alert, and picking list is wrong. Take the time to count before you go live.
5
Stock Movement — How Quantities Change

Stock In (Increases)

  • Receiving a Purchase Order — the primary method for adding stock
  • Manual adjustment (Inventory > Adjustments) — for corrections, donations, or non-PO receipts
  • Transfer from another warehouse — moves stock between locations

Stock Out (Decreases)

  • Adding a tracked Part to a ticket's Parts & Labor tab — the primary consumption method
  • Manual adjustment — for write-offs, damaged goods, or corrections
  • Transfer to another warehouse
💡 Pro TipRun a weekly inventory variance report in Analytics: compare expected stock (opening + received - used) to actual physical count. Small variances catch process problems early before they compound into large discrepancies.
6
Assets — Customer-Installed Equipment

An Asset is a specific piece of equipment installed at a customer's location that you service and maintain over time. Assets are the customer-facing counterpart to inventory — where inventory tracks stock in your possession, an Asset tracks equipment that has been installed and is now the customer's responsibility to maintain.

Assets live on the customer profile under the Service tab > Assets sub-tab. When you sell and install a Part from a ticket, you can convert it to an Asset on the customer's account, automatically creating the service record.

Asset FieldDescription
Asset NameDescriptive name: 'Cisco Router — Main Office'
Serial NumberManufacturer serial number for warranty and support tracking
Model NumberEquipment model for parts ordering and documentation
ManufacturerEquipment maker — links to manufacturer record
Install DateWhen this equipment was installed
Warranty ExpiryWhen the warranty expires — triggers alerts if configured
LocationWhere at the customer site this asset lives
StatusActive, Decommissioned, Under Warranty, Out of Service
CustomerWhich customer owns this asset
Associated TicketsAll service tickets worked on this asset — auto-populated

Every time a ticket is worked on an asset and the asset is linked under the 'Items Serviced' tab, a new entry is added to that asset's service history. Over time, this builds a complete lifecycle record for the equipment.

▲ Platform ImpactFor businesses that do recurring maintenance, assets are essential. Without them, you have no way to see how often a piece of equipment has been serviced, what work was done each time, or whether it's approaching end of life. With assets, you can spot patterns, plan proactive replacements, and have data-driven conversations with customers about maintenance investments.

Action Items

  • Enable inventory tracking in System Settings
  • Enable 'Track Inventory' only on physical Parts in the catalog
  • Create all warehouse locations before entering stock
  • Conduct a physical stock count and enter opening quantities
  • Set reorder points on high-turnover items
  • Set preferred vendors on catalog items for faster PO creation
  • Begin creating assets for equipment installed at customer sites
  • Establish team habits: receive POs on arrival, add parts to tickets when used
