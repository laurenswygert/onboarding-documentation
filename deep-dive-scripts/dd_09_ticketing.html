1
Tickets — The Operational Heartbeat of PSA

Everything in Rev.io PSA either feeds into tickets or flows out of them. Users are assigned to tickets. Customer records are linked on tickets. Catalog items are added to tickets and billed. Time is logged on tickets. Assets are serviced on tickets. Purchase orders are created from tickets. Contracts apply their rates to tickets. When tickets close, their charges go to billing.

A well-understood ticket workflow is the difference between a PSA that runs your business and one that creates friction. This session covers every field, every tab, every workflow state, and every integration point on a ticket.

→ Connects ToTickets connect to nearly every module: Customers & Contacts (required), User Management (assignment), Product Catalog (Parts & Labor charges), Time Logs (fed to billing and reporting), Contracts (rates applied), Assets (Items Serviced), Purchase Orders (created from ticket), Billing (charges flow on close), Projects (tickets organized into phases), Scheduling (dispatched from dispatch board), and Analytics (all reporting dimensions).

3
Every Ticket Tab — Complete Reference

Activity Tab

The Activity tab is the complete communication and event log for the ticket. Every action taken on the ticket creates a timestamped entry here.

  • Internal Notes: Visible only to your team. No notification sent to the customer. Use for troubleshooting notes, internal instructions, escalation context, or anything the customer shouldn't see.
  • Public Notes: Visible to the customer and appear on the customer portal. If 'Email out public activity' is enabled in System Settings, the customer receives an email when you add one.
  • Email: Inbound emails from the customer via email-to-ticket appear here. You can reply directly from this tab.
  • Signature Capture: The customer's digital signature on the completed work order is stored here, timestamped and attached to the ticket permanently.
  • File Attachments: Photos, documents, screenshots, or any file type can be attached to activity entries.
💡 Pro TipTrain techs to add a public note when they arrive on site, another when they diagnose the issue, and a final one when work is complete before capturing the signature. This creates a clean record the customer can see and reference.

Parts & Labor Tab

This is where billable charges are added to the ticket. Items are selected from the product catalog. When the ticket is closed and sent to billing, every item on this tab becomes an unposted charge on the customer's account.

  • Adding items: Search the catalog by name, SKU, or category. Select the item, set quantity. Price and description populate from the catalog.
  • Overriding price: You can manually override the unit price for this instance without changing the catalog. Useful for one-time discounts or special pricing.
  • Marking non-billable: Items can be marked non-billable (warranty work, goodwill adjustments) — they remain on the ticket for tracking but don't generate a charge.
  • Tax overrides: Individual line items can have tax toggled on or off regardless of catalog settings.
  • Notes per line: Add a note to any line item that appears on the invoice next to that charge.

Time Logs Tab

The Time Logs tab is where hours are recorded against the ticket. Time logs feed both billing (if billable) and timesheet reporting.

  • Timer: Built-in timer lets techs clock in and out while actively working on the ticket. Time is recorded to the second and converted to hours.
  • Manual entry: Techs can manually enter hours if they forgot to run the timer or are entering time retroactively.
  • Labor item: Each time entry must select a labor catalog item (On-Site Labor, Remote Support, etc.). This determines how the time is billed and categorized in reports.
  • Time clock reason: The reason category configured in System Settings.
  • Billable toggle: Time can be marked billable or non-billable.
  • Description: A text note describing what was done during this time period. Appears on invoices if time is billable.

Tasks Tab

Tasks are structured to-do items attached to the ticket. Unlike activity notes, tasks have an explicit 'complete' state and can be assigned to a specific user.

  • Task name: The action to be completed ('Replace failed drive', 'Test connectivity after replacement', 'Capture customer signature')
  • Assigned to: Which team member is responsible for completing this task
  • Due date: Optional. When the task should be completed.
  • Complete toggle: Marks the task done. Completed tasks stay on the ticket as a record.

Purchase Orders Tab

Any Purchase Orders created for this job are linked here. You can create new POs from this tab, see the status of existing ones, and track whether ordered parts have been received.

Addresses Tab

The service location for this specific job. May differ from the customer's primary billing address. This address can be used for routing and mapping by field techs, and appears on work orders.

Items Serviced Tab

Link the specific assets worked on during this ticket. Every asset linked here gets a new entry in its service history, timestamped to this ticket. This is how the complete service history for a piece of equipment is built over time.

4
Ticket Statuses and Workflow

Tickets move through statuses as work progresses. Statuses are configured in Admin > System Tables. The typical lifecycle is: New → In Progress → Pending (waiting on parts or customer) → Resolved → Closed.

Status changes are logged automatically in the ticket's activity feed with a timestamp. This creates a full audit trail of when the ticket entered each stage.

Closing a Ticket

When work is complete and the ticket is ready to bill, the final step is closing it. From the Stage panel on the right, move the status to Closed (or your equivalent final status). You'll be prompted to confirm any billing action — whether to send the ticket's charges to billing, keep them open, or mark the ticket as non-billable.

⚠ Watch OutClosing a ticket is what triggers billing. If a ticket is closed before all Parts & Labor are added, those missed items won't appear on the invoice. Train techs to add all charges before closing.
5
Email-to-Ticket Integration

Email-to-ticket automatically converts inbound emails to your support mailbox into tickets in PSA. Once configured, the workflow is entirely automatic: customer emails your support address → PSA creates a ticket → ticket is assigned to the default user group → the email becomes the first activity note on the ticket.

Configuration

Configure under Admin > Email Settings. You'll connect your support mailbox using IMAP/SMTP credentials. PSA will poll the mailbox for new emails on a regular interval.

Customer Matching

When a ticket is created from email, PSA attempts to match the sender to a customer account using the email domain. If 'Auto-assign by email domain' is enabled and the sender's domain matches a registered customer, the ticket is linked automatically. If no match is found, the ticket is created unlinked and must be manually associated with a customer.

Reply Behavior

When your team adds a public activity note to the ticket, if the customer's email is on the ticket, they can receive that note as an email reply. The conversation stays in PSA while the customer experiences it as normal email.

💡 Pro TipSet up a dedicated support email address just for PSA integration — something like support@yourcompany.com. Keep it separate from general mailboxes. This prevents personal emails from accidentally creating tickets.
6
Timesheets

The Timesheet view consolidates all time logged by your team, organized by user and week. Navigate to Service > Timesheets.

  • View by user: See all hours logged by a specific team member in a given week across all their tickets.
  • Approve time: Managers can review and approve time entries before they're locked for billing.
  • Add missing time: Techs can add time entries directly on the timesheet if they forgot to log on the ticket.
  • Billable vs. non-billable breakdown: See at a glance how much of each tech's time is billable.
  • Export: Timesheet data can be exported for payroll processing.
💡 Pro TipTimesheets are most powerful when techs log time consistently throughout the day rather than in bulk at the end of the week. End-of-week time entries are always less accurate than real-time logging. Make the timer your team's default habit.

Action Items

  • Create several real tickets to practice the full workflow
  • Have every team member log time on a ticket using both the timer and manual entry
  • Configure email-to-ticket if you have a support mailbox
  • Review your ticket status list and confirm it matches your actual workflow stages
  • Train techs on public vs. internal notes — this is where most mistakes happen
  • Build a standard closing checklist: all parts added, time logged, signature captured, then close
