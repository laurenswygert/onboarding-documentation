1
The Quote-to-Cash Flow

Quoting in Rev.io PSA is the front end of your revenue cycle. A customer expresses interest, you build a quote from your catalog, send it as a proposal for electronic acceptance, and when they say yes — the accepted quote converts directly into a ticket, a contract, or an invoice. No re-keying data between systems. The price your customer agreed to is the same price that hits the invoice.

→ Connects ToQuoting connects: Customers (quotes belong to a customer), Product Catalog (line items), Packages (bundled offerings), Quote Templates (document formatting), Opportunities (pipeline tracking), Contracts (a quote can convert to a contract), Tickets (a quote can convert to a ticket pre-loaded with line items), and Billing (a quote can convert directly to an invoice).
2
Opportunities — Pipeline Management

An Opportunity represents potential work that hasn't been confirmed. It's the stage before a formal quote — a deal you're working on, tracking, and expecting to close. Opportunities let you manage your sales pipeline inside PSA.

FieldDescription
Opportunity NameWhat the potential work is
CustomerWho the opportunity is with
Estimated ValueExpected revenue if won
Expected Close DateWhen you expect the decision
StageWhere in the sales process: Qualification, Proposal, Negotiation, Closed Won, Closed Lost
ProbabilityLikelihood of winning (%)
Assigned ToWho owns this opportunity
NotesContext, history, next steps

Opportunities are optional — you can create quotes without them. But if you track a sales pipeline and want revenue forecasting, Opportunities are where that happens.

❓ PromptDo you want to track your pipeline in PSA? If so, let's talk about your sales stages and create the opportunity stages that match your process.
3
Building a Quote — Full Field Reference

From Customer > Quotes tab > Add Quote.

FieldDescriptionNotes
Quote NameInternal name for this quoteSpecific: 'Network Installation — Main Office' not 'Quote 1'
Quote DateDate the quote is createdDefaults to today
Expiration DateWhen this quote is no longer validAppears on the customer-facing proposal. 'Valid for 30 days' is standard.
Quote TemplateWhich template formats the proposalMust be configured in Admin > Quote Templates first
OpportunityLink to an existing opportunityOptional. Ties this quote to your pipeline tracking.
Assigned ToWho created/owns this quote
NotesInternal notes about this quoteNot customer-facing

Adding Line Items

  • From catalog: Search and select catalog items. Quantity and price populate automatically. You can override the price for this quote without changing the catalog.
  • From packages: Select a package — it expands into all component items on the quote. Customers see the individual items if you use component pricing, or just the package name if you set a flat package price.
  • Manual items: Add a line item without a catalog reference. Use sparingly — free-form items don't tie to catalog reporting.
  • Sections: Organize line items into labeled groups: 'Hardware,' 'Installation,' 'First Year Support.' Makes complex quotes much more readable.
  • Line-item notes: Add a description note to any item that appears on the quote next to that line.
  • Optional items: Mark items as optional for the customer to add or remove when reviewing the proposal.
  • Discounts: Apply a percentage or fixed discount at the line-item level or as a quote-level subtotal discount.
4
Proposals — Sending and Acceptance

A Proposal is the customer-facing version of a quote. From the quote, click Add Proposal.

FieldDescription
Recipient(s)The contact(s) who will receive and can accept the proposal
MessageCustom email message sent with the proposal link
ExpirationWhen the proposal link expires
Require SignatureWhether the customer must sign to accept
Require PaymentWhether a deposit is required on acceptance

Once sent, the customer receives an email with a link to view the proposal online. From the proposal page, they can: review all line items, select or deselect optional items, sign electronically, and accept or decline.

When the customer accepts, the proposal status updates in real time. The acceptance is time-stamped, linked to their account permanently, and the contact who accepted is recorded.

💡 Pro TipSend a test proposal to yourself before using with real customers. Verify the formatting, logo, line items, and footer language all look exactly right from the customer's perspective.
5
Converting an Accepted Quote

After acceptance, convert the quote to the appropriate next step:

Convert ToWhen to UseWhat Happens
TicketService-based work that needs to be scheduled and trackedCreates a new ticket pre-loaded with all quoted line items on the Parts & Labor tab
ContractOngoing service agreementCreates a contract record pre-configured with the agreement terms
InvoiceUpfront payment or completed workCreates a draft invoice with all quoted line items. Bill immediately.
⚠ Watch OutConverting a quote doesn't delete it — the quote and proposal history stay on the customer record permanently. This is your evidence of what was agreed to and when.

Action Items

  • Set up at least one Quote Template in Admin > Quote Templates
  • Create Opportunity stages that match your sales process if using pipeline tracking
  • Build a quote for a real upcoming job end-to-end
  • Send a test proposal to yourself
  • Document your standard quote expiration period and put it in the template footer
  • Train your team on the optional items feature for customizable proposals
