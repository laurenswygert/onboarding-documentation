Automating Predictable Revenue

Recurring Services are Rev.io PSA's automation engine for your predictable, repeating charges. Monthly managed service fees, quarterly maintenance charges, annual software subscriptions, weekly monitoring fees — set them up once and PSA generates the charge automatically on the schedule you define. No manual creation. No missed billing. No human error.

This is typically the highest-ROI automation in the platform for businesses with recurring revenue. The time savings compound every month, and the revenue protection from never missing a recurring charge compounds over the life of every customer relationship.

→ Connects ToRecurring Services generate Unposted charges in the Billing module on their scheduled dates. They reference catalog items for the charge description and pricing. They can optionally be linked to a Contract (the recurring charge is part of the agreement). Generated charges are reviewed, posted, and rolled into invoices through the standard billing workflow.
▲ Platform ImpactIf recurring services aren't configured before go-live, your team will manually create billing charges every month for every recurring customer. At 20 customers with one recurring charge each, that's 20 manual transactions every month. At 200 customers, it's 200. At some point it becomes a material operational risk — charges get missed, billing is late, and revenue is lost.

3
Managing the Charge Generation Cycle

How Charges Are Generated

On the Next Bill Date, PSA automatically creates an Unposted charge on the customer's Billing tab. This happens on a regular system schedule — you don't need to trigger it manually. The charge references the catalog item, quantity, and price configured on the recurring service.

Reviewing Unposted Charges

Navigate to the customer's Billing tab > Unposted sub-tab to see pending charges. Review each charge: verify the amount, description, and that it belongs to this billing period. When satisfied, post the charges. Posting moves them to the Ledger and makes them available for invoicing.

Bulk Review

From the main Billing module, you can view all Unposted charges across all customers simultaneously — useful for end-of-month billing runs when you're processing many customers at once.

💡 Pro TipSchedule your recurring service dates strategically. If all your recurring services bill on the 1st of the month, you have a very predictable monthly rhythm. If they're scattered throughout the month, you're doing billing review continuously. Standardize on one or two billing dates if possible.
4
Editing, Pausing, and Cancelling
  • Editing: Open the recurring service record and update quantity, price, or frequency. Changes take effect on the next bill date.
  • Pausing: If a customer's service is temporarily suspended, set the Next Bill Date to a future date when billing should resume.
  • Cancelling: Deactivate the recurring service. No future charges will generate. Historical charges remain on the ledger.
  • Price increases: Update the unit price on the recurring service record before the next bill date. The new price applies to the next charge and all subsequent ones.
⚠ Watch OutIf you update a recurring service price mid-cycle and the charge has already generated as Unposted, you'll need to manually edit the Unposted charge for this period. The updated price only applies to future auto-generated charges.

Action Items

  • List every customer with a recurring charge you currently manage manually
  • Set up a recurring service for each one in PSA
  • Review every Next Bill Date to confirm it aligns with your billing schedule
  • Link each recurring service to its corresponding contract where applicable
  • Decide on in advance vs. in arrears and apply consistently
  • Standardize billing dates to simplify your monthly billing process
