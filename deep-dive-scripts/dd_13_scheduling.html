Scheduling — Calendar View vs. Dispatch Board

Rev.io PSA has two scheduling interfaces. The Calendar View is a personal schedule view — it shows one user's events and appointments in a day/week/month format. The Dispatch Board is the operational scheduling tool — it shows your entire team side by side with unscheduled tickets queued up ready to assign.

The Dispatch Board is what replaces a physical whiteboard, shared spreadsheet, or phone-based dispatch. It gives dispatchers a real-time view of every technician's schedule and a drag-and-drop interface for assigning work.

→ Connects ToThe Dispatch Board pulls from: Users and User Groups (who appears on the board), Tickets (unscheduled work queue), Business Hours from System Settings (availability windows), Customer Addresses (location context for field routing), and Calendar Events (existing commitments). When a ticket is dispatched, a Calendar Event is created on the technician's schedule, and the tech sees it immediately in the mobile app.
2
The Dispatch Board — Full Interface Guide

Board Layout

  • Technician columns: Each active technician (or each visible user in the selected group) has a column. Their calendar color from User Management is reflected here.
  • Time rows: The vertical axis is time, divided into intervals. Your business hours are highlighted; outside hours are visually muted.
  • Unscheduled ticket panel: Open tickets without a scheduled time slot appear in a sidebar. These are filterable by ticket type, priority, user group, and customer.
  • Existing events: Scheduled events and appointments already on a tech's calendar appear as color-coded blocks in their column.
  • Group filter: Show only technicians in a specific user group — essential when managing large teams or multiple territory groups.

Dispatching a Ticket

  • Find the ticket in the unscheduled panel (filter if needed)
  • Drag it to the time slot on the appropriate technician's column
  • A confirmation dialog appears — confirm start time, duration, and add notes for the tech
  • Save — the event appears on the board and immediately on the tech's mobile app schedule

3
Technician Availability Configuration

The dispatch board respects several availability configurations:

  • Company Business Hours (System Settings): Defines when the board shows active time slots for all users by default.
  • User-level Business Hours: Set on each user's profile if their hours differ from the company default. A part-time tech or a tech in a different time zone gets their own hours.
  • Existing Calendar Events: Any events already on a tech's calendar show as occupied — the board displays them as busy blocks preventing double-booking.
  • User Groups: Filter the board to show only the relevant team. A dispatcher managing field techs doesn't need to see office staff on the board.

The Calendar View — Personal Scheduling

The Calendar view is the individual scheduling interface. Each user can see their own calendar, and managers can view other users' calendars. Access it from the Scheduling module > Calendar view.

Creating Events Manually

Click any time slot on the calendar (or use Add Event) to create a scheduled event. Fields include:

SubjectBrief description shown on the calendar grid

5
Tasks — Action Items on a Schedule

Tasks are to-do items with due dates and assignees. They're different from calendar events (which have a specific time slot) and tickets (which represent customer service requests). Tasks are internal action items: 'Follow up with vendor on PO status,' 'Prepare proposal for Thursday meeting,' 'Complete compliance training by Friday.'

Tasks appear in the Scheduling module's task list and on each user's personal task view. They can be assigned to any user, given a due date, and linked to a customer or ticket for context.

💡 Pro TipTasks are underutilized in most PSA implementations. They're the best place for non-ticket work that would otherwise live in someone's email or personal task manager — keeping all work in one system.
6
Mobile App — Field Technician Experience

The Rev.io PSA mobile app is the field technician's interface for the dispatch board. Once a ticket is dispatched to a tech, it appears on their mobile schedule immediately. From their phone, the tech can:

  • Accept or acknowledge the dispatch
  • View all ticket details, customer information, and job notes
  • Navigate to the service address (opens mapping app)
  • Update the ticket status (Traveling, On-Site, Work Complete)
  • Log time using the built-in timer
  • Add activity notes (public or internal)
  • Take photos and attach them to the ticket
  • Add parts used from the catalog to the Parts & Labor tab
  • Capture customer signature on job completion
  • View their full schedule for the day

Everything the tech does in the mobile app syncs back to PSA in real time. Dispatchers see status updates without calling the tech. Managers see time logs as they're recorded. Customers receive notifications when public notes are added.
