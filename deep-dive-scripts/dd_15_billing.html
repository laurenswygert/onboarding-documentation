1
The Billing Module — Revenue Collection Hub

The Billing module is where all the work your team has done becomes money. Every ticket your techs close, every recurring service that fires, every contract fee that generates — it all flows here as an Unposted charge, waits for review, gets posted to the Ledger, and becomes an invoice that goes to the customer.

Understanding the billing workflow — where charges come from, how they move through the system, and how to manage exceptions — is critical for anyone who processes billing in Rev.io PSA.

→ Connects ToBilling receives charges from: Tickets (Parts & Labor when a ticket is sent to billing), Recurring Services (auto-generated on billing dates), Contracts (flat fees from billing parameters), and Manual Charges (added directly on a customer). From Billing, charges flow to Invoices which go to customers. Payments come back and are applied to invoices on the Ledger.
2
Charge Sources — How Money Enters Billing
SourceHow It WorksWhere to Find It
Ticket Parts & LaborWhen a ticket is closed and 'Send to Billing' is selected, all items on the Parts & Labor tab become chargesCustomer > Billing tab > Unposted after ticket close
Recurring ServicesAuto-generated on the configured Next Bill Date. No manual trigger neededCustomer > Billing tab > Unposted on the billing date
Contract Billing ParametersGenerated on the contract's billing schedule — flat recurring feeCustomer > Billing tab > Unposted on the contract billing date
Manual ChargesAdded directly by a billing adminCustomer profile > Quick Actions > Add Charge

All charges — regardless of source — start as Unposted. This is intentional. The Unposted tab is your review checkpoint before anything is finalized and sent to a customer.

3
The Unposted Tab — Review and Post

Navigate to Customer > Billing tab > Unposted sub-tab. Every pending charge for this customer lives here.

What You Can Do From Unposted

  • Review: Verify each charge — description, quantity, unit price, total, tax treatment
  • Edit: Change quantity, price, or description on any unposted charge before it's finalized
  • Delete: Remove a charge that shouldn't be billed (warranty work, error, goodwill)
  • Add: Add a charge that was missed — select from the catalog or enter manually
  • Post individually: Post specific charges while leaving others in Unposted for further review
  • Post all: Post all pending charges for this customer at once

Posting moves charges to the Ledger. Once posted, a charge can still be corrected — but it requires a Credit Memo, which creates a paper trail. The easiest correction is always before posting.

❓ PromptDo you want to review every charge before posting, or are you comfortable auto-posting certain types? For example, recurring service charges are predictable — many clients auto-post those but manually review ticket-based charges.
💡 Pro TipBuild a pre-billing review checklist: before posting any charges for the month, run the 'Unposted Charges' report in Analytics to see everything pending across all customers. Catch missing charges and errors before they become customer-facing problems.
4
The Ledger — The Complete Financial Record

The Ledger is the immutable financial record of a customer account. Every transaction in chronological order: charges, invoices, payments, credits, adjustments. It never disappears — even voided invoices remain as void records.

The Ledger shows the customer's current balance at the top. Below it, every line tells a story: a charge was posted on this date, an invoice was created, the customer paid on this date, a credit was applied.

When a customer calls to dispute a charge or question a payment, the Ledger is your first and usually only stop. Every transaction is traceable back to its source — you can click through from a Ledger entry to the original ticket, the original recurring service, or the original invoice.

💡 Pro TipPrint or export the Ledger when a customer disputes billing. Having the complete documented history — timestamped, with every transaction referenced — resolves most billing disputes quickly and professionally.
5
Manual Charges and Credits

Adding a Manual Charge

From the customer profile > Quick Actions > Add Charge. Select a catalog item or enter a description and price manually. The charge appears immediately in Unposted.

Issuing a Credit Memo

A credit memo reduces the customer's balance. Use it to correct a billing error on a posted charge, apply a goodwill credit, or acknowledge a refund. From the customer's Billing tab, find the posted charge or invoice and issue a credit against it. The credit appears on the Ledger as a negative amount, reducing the outstanding balance.

Rebilling

If a ticket was closed and billed incorrectly — wrong items, wrong quantities, wrong prices — you can rebill it. From the ticket, use the Rebill option to pull the charges back to Unposted, make corrections, and re-post them. The Ledger records the full history of the original charge and the rebill.

⚠ Watch OutRebilling creates complexity on the customer's Ledger. It's the right tool for significant errors, but for small adjustments, a Credit Memo is cleaner and simpler. Reserve rebilling for cases where the original charge is materially wrong.

Action Items

  • Close a real ticket and trace its charges through to Unposted
  • Practice posting charges and reviewing them on the Ledger
  • Issue a test Credit Memo to understand the correction workflow
  • Define your billing cycle: how often will you post and invoice?
  • Decide which charge types to auto-post vs. manually review
  • Run the Unposted Charges report before each billing cycle
