1
Payment Collection in Rev.io PSA

Connecting a payment processor to Rev.io PSA eliminates the manual gap between sending an invoice and receiving payment. Customers can pay directly on their invoice, autopay collects recurring charges automatically, and every payment records to the Ledger without manual entry. For businesses with recurring revenue, this automation can save dozens of hours per month.

→ Connects ToPayment Processors connect to: Customer Billing (Payment Accounts stored per customer), Invoices (customers pay directly from the invoice link), Autopay (processor charges the stored method automatically on the billing date), Ledger (all electronic payments record automatically), and the Customer Portal (self-service payment).
2
Connecting a Processor

Navigate to Admin > Payment Settings. Available processors depend on your account configuration and region. Common integrations include Stripe and Commerce Hub.

Connection Steps

  • Log into your payment processor's admin portal and locate your API credentials (API Key, Merchant ID, or equivalent)
  • In PSA Admin > Payment Settings, select your processor
  • Enter the API credentials exactly as provided — these are case-sensitive
  • Save and confirm the connection — PSA will validate the credentials
  • Run a test transaction to confirm end-to-end functionality before going live
⚠ Watch OutNever attempt to configure payment processor integration with guessed or incomplete credentials. An incorrect setup could result in payment failures or, in rare cases, test transactions being processed against real accounts. Have credentials in hand before starting.
❓ PromptDo you have your payment processor credentials available today? If yes, let's connect now. If not, let's schedule a follow-up session specifically for this.
3
Payment Accounts — Storing Customer Payment Methods

Once the processor is connected, you can store payment methods on customer accounts. Navigate to Customer > Billing tab > Payment Accounts > Add Payment Account.

FieldDescription
Payment Method TypeCredit Card or ACH/Bank Account
Card Number / Account DetailsEntered and immediately tokenized — PSA never stores raw card data
Billing NameName on the card or account
Billing AddressAddress associated with the payment method
DefaultWhether this is the primary payment method for autopay and one-click payments
LabelInternal label: 'Corporate Visa ending 4521'

PSA stores a secure token from your payment processor — not the actual card number. This means PSA is not in scope for PCI DSS card storage requirements.

❓ PromptWhich customers should have payment methods stored? Start with your recurring service customers — those are the ones who will benefit most from autopay.
4
Processing Payments

One-Time Payment

Process a payment on demand from Customer profile > Quick Actions > Add Payment. Select the amount, the stored payment method, and the invoice(s) to apply the payment to. The transaction processes immediately and records to the Ledger.

Payment from Invoice

From any open invoice, a 'Pay Now' option lets you collect payment directly against that specific invoice. Useful when a customer calls to pay a bill over the phone.

Customer Self-Pay via Portal

Customers with portal access can view their invoices and pay them directly online using a stored payment method or by entering a new card. Portal payments record automatically with no manual entry required.

5
Autopay — Automatic Payment Collection

Autopay charges a customer's stored payment method automatically when an invoice is generated or when it reaches its due date.

Navigate to Customer > Billing tab > Payment Accounts > Manage Autopay. Enable it, select the stored payment method, and set the trigger:

Autopay TriggerWhen It ChargesBest For
On Invoice PostCharges immediately when the invoice is postedPrepay customers, subscription businesses
On Invoice Due DateCharges on the invoice due dateNet 30 customers, allowing review time before charging
💡 Pro TipFor managed service customers on monthly billing, autopay on invoice post is ideal — the invoice is generated, posted, and paid in one workflow. The customer never needs to take action, and you never have to chase the payment.
⚠ Watch OutBefore enabling autopay for existing customers, communicate it to them first. Customers who aren't expecting to be charged automatically may dispute the transaction with their bank, resulting in a chargeback. A brief heads-up email prevents most chargeback situations.

Action Items

  • Gather API credentials from your payment processor portal
  • Complete the connection in Admin > Payment Settings
  • Run a test transaction to confirm the connection works
  • Add payment methods for all recurring service customers
  • Enable autopay for monthly managed service customers
  • Communicate the change to customers before enabling autopay
  • Configure Customer Portal payment access for self-pay customers
