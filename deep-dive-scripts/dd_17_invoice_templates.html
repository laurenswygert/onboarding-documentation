Pre-Session Checklist

  • Navigate to Admin > Invoice Templates
  • Have company logo file ready (PNG or JPG)
  • Know payment terms, late fee language, and footer content
  • Know if separate templates are needed for different brands or customer types
1
Templates — Your Brand on Every Document

Every invoice and quote that goes to a customer is formatted by a template. The template controls your logo, company header, line item layout, subtotals, tax display, and footer. Configure it once and it applies to every document automatically.

→ Connects ToInvoice Templates are used whenever: an invoice is generated (individual or batch), a credit memo is created, or a work order document is produced. Quote Templates are used when a proposal is sent to a customer. Both template types reference your company information from System Settings.
2
Building an Invoice Template

Navigate to Admin > Invoice Templates. Edit the default or create a new one.

Header Section

  • Logo: Upload your company logo. PNG or JPG at 150+ DPI for crisp rendering. Position top-left or top-center.
  • Company Name and Address: Auto-populated from System Settings. Verify it matches.
  • Contact Information: Phone number, email, and website for the company header.
  • Document Title: The label at the top of the document — 'Invoice', 'Tax Invoice', 'Service Invoice'. Change to match your business context.

Line Item Layout

  • Columns to display: Description, Quantity, Unit Price, Total are standard. Add or remove columns based on what your customers expect to see.
  • Group by category: Option to group line items by catalog category on the invoice.
  • Show item notes: Whether per-line notes from the Parts & Labor tab appear on the invoice.
  • Show tax per line: Whether tax is broken out per item or shown as a single subtotal.

Footer Section

The footer is the legal and payment section of every document. It should include:

  • Payment terms: 'Net 30,' 'Due on Receipt,' 'Payment due within 30 days of invoice date'
  • Accepted payment methods: 'We accept Visa, Mastercard, ACH, and check'
  • Late fee language: 'Invoices past due are subject to a 1.5% monthly finance charge'
  • Billing contact: 'Questions? Contact billing@yourcompany.com or call 555-1234'
  • Thank you message: 'Thank you for your business'
  • Legal disclaimers if required by your industry or jurisdiction
❓ PromptWhat do you want in your invoice footer? Walk me through your payment terms, late fee policy, and any legal language you need to include.
3
Quote Templates

Navigate to Admin > Quote Templates. The configuration process is identical to Invoice Templates but the content is different — this is a sales document, not a billing document.

Quote-Specific Footer Content

  • Quote validity: 'This estimate is valid for 30 days from the date above'
  • Acceptance instructions: 'To accept this quote, click Accept on the proposal link or contact us to confirm'
  • Scope notes: 'This quote covers the items listed. Additional work not listed is out of scope and will be quoted separately'
  • Commencement language: 'Work will begin within 5 business days of written acceptance'
❓ PromptShould your quote footer language differ from your invoice footer? Most clients want separate messaging — quotes are sales documents, invoices are billing documents.
4
Multiple Templates

You can create multiple invoice templates and multiple quote templates. This is useful when:

  • You have multiple brands or DBAs and need different logos and company names on documents
  • You have different customer types that require different formats (consumer vs. enterprise)
  • Your invoices for different service types look meaningfully different
  • You have bilingual customers requiring documents in different languages

When creating an invoice or selecting a quote template, you choose which template to use. The default template applies automatically unless overridden.

💡 Pro TipCreate a 'Template Review' step in your go-live checklist. Generate a test invoice and a test proposal. Share both with your finance team and your best customer relationship and ask for feedback before going live. Document layout is one of those things that's very visible to customers.

Action Items

  • Upload your company logo in the correct format
  • Finalize all footer language: payment terms, late fees, contact info, thank-you
  • Review the line item layout with a sample invoice to confirm it looks right
  • Build a Quote Template with quote-specific footer language
  • Create additional templates if needed for multiple brands or customer types
  • Generate test documents and review them before going live
