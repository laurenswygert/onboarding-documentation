1
The Invoicing Workflow

Invoicing is the final step in the billing cycle — taking posted Ledger charges and converting them into the formal invoice documents that go to customers. The workflow: charges are posted to the Ledger, grouped into an invoice, reviewed in draft, posted (finalized), and sent. The customer receives the invoice, reviews it, and pays.

→ Connects ToInvoicing connects: the Ledger (source of all invoice charges), Invoice Templates (formatting), Customer Contacts (delivery — invoices go to the Billing Contact's email), Payment Processors (customers can pay the invoice electronically), the Customer Portal (customers view and pay invoices online), and Analytics (invoice and revenue reporting).
2
Creating Invoices — Individual and Batch

Individual Invoice

Navigate to Customer > Billing tab > Invoices sub-tab > Create Invoice. PSA pulls all posted, uninvoiced Ledger charges for this customer into a draft invoice automatically. You review, adjust if needed, post, and send.

Batch Invoicing

From the main Billing module, you can filter all customers with uninvoiced posted charges and generate invoices for multiple customers simultaneously. This is the most efficient approach for a monthly billing cycle.

  • Filter customers with uninvoiced charges
  • Select all (or select specific customers)
  • Click Generate Invoices
  • PSA creates a draft invoice for each selected customer
  • Review the batch in the Invoice list
  • Post all (or post individually after review)
  • Send all (or send individually)

3
The Invoice Draft — Review Before Sending

Every invoice starts as a Draft. In Draft status you can make any change: add or remove line items, adjust dates, change the billing contact, add a memo, or apply a manual discount. Once you post the invoice, it is finalized — corrections require a Credit Memo.

Draft Review Checklist

  • All expected line items are present
  • Quantities and prices are correct
  • Invoice date and due date are correct
  • The correct billing contact is shown
  • Tax is calculated correctly
  • Any promised discounts are applied
  • Memo or special instructions added if needed
⚠ Watch OutThe most common billing mistake is posting and sending without review. A posted invoice with an error requires a Credit Memo, a customer communication, and potentially a reissued invoice. A 60-second review before posting prevents all of this.
4
Sending Invoices

Post the invoice to finalize it, then send it. PSA emails the invoice to the billing contact using your configured template. The email includes a link to view the invoice online — from that link, the customer can review, download the PDF, and pay if a processor is connected.

Delivery Options

  • Email: Default. Sent to the billing contact's email address.
  • Portal: Invoice appears in the customer's portal — customer is notified to review.
  • Print: Generate a PDF for physical mailing.
  • Multiple: Send email AND add to portal simultaneously.
💡 Pro TipIf a customer says they didn't receive an invoice, check: (1) Is the billing contact email correct on their account? (2) Did it land in their spam folder? (3) Can you resend from the invoice record? You can resend any sent invoice from the invoice's action menu.
5
Credits, Rebilling, and Write-Offs

Credit Memo

Issue a credit to reduce the customer's balance. Use when: an invoice has an error after posting, you're applying a goodwill credit, or a customer is entitled to a refund. The credit appears on the Ledger as a negative amount and can be applied to an open invoice or left as a credit on the account.

Rebilling

If a ticket was billed incorrectly, rebilling pulls the charges back to Unposted, lets you correct them, and re-generates the invoice. The Ledger records the full trail: original charge, reversal, corrected charge.

Write-Off

When a customer genuinely cannot or will not pay and you decide to stop pursuing the balance, write it off. This removes the amount from the Accounts Receivable aging report and records it as bad debt in your Ledger. Write-offs should require manager approval — document your approval process.

6
Invoice Reporting

Key invoicing reports in Analytics:

  • Accounts Receivable Aging: Outstanding invoices by age — current, 30 days, 60 days, 90+ days. Your primary collection management tool.
  • Invoice Summary: Invoices created, posted, paid, and voided in a date range.
  • Revenue by Customer: Total billed per customer — identify your top accounts.
  • Revenue by Item: Which catalog items are generating the most revenue.
  • Outstanding Invoices: All unpaid invoices with amounts and due dates.
💡 Pro TipRun the AR Aging report weekly. Invoices that reach 60+ days outstanding represent collection risk. A weekly review lets you catch them early and take action — a phone call or payment reminder — before they become write-offs.

Action Items

  • Run your first real invoice end-to-end
  • Send a test invoice to yourself to verify template and email
  • Establish your billing cycle: date, frequency, and who is responsible
  • Build a pre-send review checklist for invoice quality control
  • Set up a weekly AR Aging report review as a standing process
  • Define your approval process for credits and write-offs
