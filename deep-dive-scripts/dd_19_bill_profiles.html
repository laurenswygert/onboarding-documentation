1
What Bill Profiles Do

Bill Profiles are reusable billing rule sets. Instead of configuring payment terms, invoice delivery method, billing frequency, and late fee rules on each customer individually, you build a profile for each distinct set of rules and assign the appropriate profile to each customer.

This creates consistent, auditable billing behavior across your entire customer base. When a customer asks 'what are my payment terms?', the answer is always in their profile — no hunting through account-level settings.

→ Connects ToBill Profiles apply to every aspect of a customer's billing behavior: invoice generation frequency, payment terms (printed on invoices), invoice delivery method (email, portal, print), late fee application, and autopay configuration.
▲ Platform ImpactWithout Bill Profiles, billing behavior is set inconsistently customer by customer. Some get Net 30, some get Net 15, some never get late fees, some get invoiced monthly and some ad hoc. This inconsistency creates customer confusion, audit risk, and operational unpredictability.

Building and Assigning Profiles

Build profiles before assigning them. A good starting set for most service businesses:

Profile NamePayment TermsFrequencyLate FeeAutopay
Standard MonthlyNet 30Monthly1.5%/month after 30 daysNo
Managed Service MonthlyDue on ReceiptMonthly1.5%/month after 15 daysYes
Enterprise QuarterlyNet 45Quarterly$50 flat after 45 daysNo
Residential Due on ReceiptDue on ReceiptOn Demand$25 flat after 30 daysOptional

Once profiles exist, assign them to customers from Customer > Billing tab. Every customer should have a Bill Profile assigned before go-live.

⚠ Watch OutIf a customer doesn't have a Bill Profile assigned, PSA may fall back to system defaults that don't match your intentions. Do a pre-go-live audit: run a report of all customers without a Bill Profile assigned and assign before your first billing run.

Action Items

  • Define all distinct billing rule sets used across your customer base
  • Build a Bill Profile for each rule set
  • Assign the correct profile to every customer
  • Run a pre-go-live audit to find any customers missing a profile
  • Review late fee rules with finance or legal before enabling
