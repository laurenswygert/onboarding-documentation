1
Accounting Periods — Why They Matter

Accounting Periods define the time windows for your financial records in Rev.io PSA. Every transaction — every charge, invoice, payment, and credit — belongs to the period in which it was posted. When you close a period, its records are locked and cannot be changed retroactively.

This creates financial integrity: your numbers for March are your numbers for March, permanently. Month-over-month comparisons are reliable. Reports can be run for any closed period with confidence that the data is final. If you export to an accounting system, the data you export is the data you'll have — no post-export modifications.

→ Connects ToAccounting Periods affect all financial activity in the Billing module — every transaction belongs to an open period. Period closure coordinates with accounting integrations (QuickBooks, Sage, etc.) where the close in PSA triggers or aligns with the export to the external system. Analytics reporting by period produces the clean monthly snapshots finance teams need.
▲ Platform ImpactWithout a consistent period close process, old transactions can be backdated, month-over-month reports become unreliable, and reconciliation with your accounting system becomes a manual exercise in finding discrepancies. The period close is a small operational step with significant financial integrity implications.
2
Understanding Periods

PSA typically generates periods automatically by calendar month. Navigate to Accounting Periods to see the list: open periods, closed periods, and the current active period.

Period StatusMeaningActions Available
OpenCurrent period — transactions post herePost charges, invoices, payments, credits
ClosedFinalized — lockedView only. No new transactions can post.
FutureScheduled but not yet activeCannot post transactions — must wait for the period to open
❓ PromptDo you use calendar months, or do you operate on a non-standard fiscal calendar?
💡 Internal NoteMost clients use calendar months. If they use a non-standard fiscal year (e.g., April–March, or fiscal quarters that don't align with calendar quarters), confirm this before go-live and verify period configuration with the implementation team.
3
The Month-End Close Process

A complete month-end close in PSA should follow this sequence:

  • Review all Unposted charges and post any that belong in this period
  • Generate and post all invoices for the month
  • Confirm all payments received this period are recorded and applied
  • Run the AR Aging report — note any overdue accounts for follow-up
  • Verify the period's Ledger totals match your expected billing amounts
  • Close the period in PSA
  • If using an accounting integration — trigger the export to your accounting system

Once the period is closed, any corrections that arise must be handled in the next open period through credit memos or adjustments. The closed period record is permanent.

💡 Pro TipAssign period close ownership to one person on your finance team. When multiple people can close periods, close timing becomes inconsistent and accountability is unclear. One owner, one process, same date every month.
4
Accounting Integrations

If you sync PSA data to QuickBooks, Sage, or another accounting platform, the accounting period is the unit of synchronization. You export a period's data after closing it in PSA, and the accounting system receives finalized, locked records.

Coordinate your PSA period close date with your accounting team's close schedule. If accounting closes books on the 5th of the following month, close your PSA period by the 4th to give them clean data.

❓ PromptAre you using an accounting integration? If so, what system, and when does your accounting team close their books each month?
⚠ Watch OutIf you post transactions in PSA after you've already exported a closed period to your accounting system, those transactions won't be in the accounting system without a manual re-export or reconciliation entry. The PSA close and accounting close must stay synchronized.

Action Items

  • Confirm your accounting period structure — calendar month or custom
  • Assign one person as the period close owner
  • Build a written month-end close checklist with each step and who does it
  • Align your PSA close date with your accounting team's schedule
  • If using an integration, confirm the export timing and process
  • Run the first period close as a dry run before going fully live
