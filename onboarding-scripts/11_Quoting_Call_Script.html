1
How Quoting Fits Into the Sales-to-Service Flow

Quoting in Rev.io PSA closes the loop between your sales process and your service delivery. A customer expresses interest, you build a quote using items from your catalog, you send a proposal for electronic acceptance, and when they say yes — the accepted quote converts directly into a ticket, a contract, or an invoice. No re-entering data. No copying from one system to another.

This is important because it means the pricing your customer agreed to is the same pricing that flows into billing. There's no gap where a tech might charge differently than what was quoted.

→ Connects ToQuoting pulls from: the Product Catalog and Packages (line items), Quote Templates (document formatting), and Customer records (who you're quoting). When a quote is accepted and converted, it creates either a Ticket (in the Service module), a Contract (in the customer's Service tab), or an Invoice (in Billing). The full quote history stays on the customer's Quotes tab.
2
Opportunities (Optional Pipeline Tracking)

Opportunities represent potential work that isn't confirmed yet — a lead, a project discussion, a renewal conversation. They're the top of your sales funnel and let you track pipeline and forecast revenue inside PSA.

They're optional. If you don't need to track pipeline, you can skip opportunities and go straight to building quotes. If you do use them, you'll find them under Customer > Quotes tab > Opportunities sub-tab.

❓ PromptDo you want to track your sales pipeline in PSA, or will you go straight to building quotes without the opportunity stage?
3
Building a Quote

From a customer > Quotes tab > Add Quote. Give it a name, link it to an opportunity if using them, and select a quote template. Then add line items from the catalog. You can also add entire packages, which expand into all their component items.

Additional options as you build: organize items into sections, apply line-item or quote-level discounts, mark items as optional for the customer, and add notes or descriptions to any line item.

❓ PromptLet's build a quote together — pick a customer and something you frequently quote.
▲ Platform ImpactThe quote builder pulling from your catalog is what makes pricing consistent. If catalog items and packages are well-built, quoting a complex job can take minutes instead of hours. The investment you made in the Product Catalog and Packages pays off here.
4
Proposals & Acceptance

Once the quote is built, create a Proposal — the customer-facing version. From the quote, click Add Proposal, select the contact who will receive and sign it, and hit Send. The customer gets an email with a link to view the quote online.

From that link, the customer can review the quote, see optional items, and electronically accept or decline. When they accept, the status updates in real time and you see it immediately. The acceptance is time-stamped and stored permanently on the customer record.

❓ PromptDo your customers typically approve work before it starts? Let's send a test proposal to yourself so you can see the customer experience firsthand.

When a proposal is accepted, convert it to: a Ticket for service-based work, a Contract for an ongoing agreement, or an Invoice if billing upfront. The conversion carries the line items and pricing over automatically.

Action Items

  • Create a quote for a real upcoming job to practice
  • Send a test proposal to yourself to experience the customer view
  • Decide your standard conversion path: quote to ticket, contract, or invoice
