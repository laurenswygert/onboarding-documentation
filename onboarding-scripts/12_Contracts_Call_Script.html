1
What Contracts Control and Why They Matter

Contracts in Rev.io PSA are the formal record of a service agreement between you and a customer. They define what you've committed to deliver, the pricing that applies to work under that agreement, and how the customer gets billed. They're the bridge between your sales agreements and your ongoing service delivery.

When a contract is active on a customer account, it does three important things: it applies your contracted rates to tickets worked under that agreement, it generates recurring billing charges on schedule, and it tracks all work history — time, parts, tickets — so you always know what's been used against the contract.

→ Connects ToContracts connect to: Customer records (where they live), the Product Catalog (contract rates reference catalog items), Billing (contract billing parameters generate recurring charges), Tickets (work is tracked against the contract), Time Logs (hours are tracked against the contract), and Recurring Services (often work together with contracts for comprehensive managed service billing).
▲ Platform ImpactWithout contracts set up, your managed service customers get billed at standard catalog rates instead of their agreed-upon rates. Work that's supposed to be included gets billed. Work that should be at a special rate gets billed wrong. Contracts are what make the billing correct automatically.
2
Creating a Contract

From the customer account > Service tab > Contracts sub-tab > Add Contract. Key fields:

  • Contract Name — something descriptive that clearly identifies this agreement.
  • Start Date and End Date — the contract term. Used for renewal reporting and SLA management.
  • Contract Type — how work is delivered and billed. Managed Service (flat fee covers certain work), Block Hours (a pre-purchased bank of time), Time & Materials (billed for actual work performed), or Warranty (work covered at no charge).
  • Status — only Active contracts are applied to tickets automatically.
  • Assigned User / User Group — the team responsible for this contract.
❓ PromptWhat types of agreements do you have with your contract customers — flat monthly fee, block of hours, or billed for actual work done?
3
Contract Rates

Contract Rates are the pricing rules that apply when work is done under this contract. Navigate to the Contract Rates tab. For each type of work this contract covers, you add the catalog item and specify:

  • The rate that applies under this contract — which may be lower than your standard catalog price
  • Whether that item is billable at the contracted rate, or included and covered at no charge

For example: 'Remote support is included in this contract — no charge. On-site labor is billed at $95/hour instead of the standard $125.' Both of those rules get set up here as contract rates.

❓ PromptAre there services that are fully included in the contract at no charge? And are there others that are billed at a special rate?
4
Billing Parameters

The Billing Parameters tab is where you set up any recurring fee associated with this contract. If the customer pays a flat monthly amount for the agreement itself — in addition to or instead of per-service charges — you configure that here. Set the amount, the billing frequency, and when it should start. PSA will generate that charge automatically on schedule.

❓ PromptDoes this contract have a recurring monthly fee? Or does the customer only pay for services as they're delivered?

The Documentation tab lets you attach the signed agreement, SOWs, or any supporting files directly to the contract record so everything is in one place.

Action Items

  • Create a contract for every active managed service or maintenance customer
  • Set contract rates to exactly match your agreement terms
  • Configure billing parameters for any flat recurring fees
  • Attach the signed agreement under the Documentation tab
