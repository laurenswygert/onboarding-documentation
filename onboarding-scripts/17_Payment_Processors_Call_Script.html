1
Why Payment Processors Matter to the Billing Workflow

Connecting a payment processor to Rev.io PSA closes the loop on your billing workflow. Right now, when you send an invoice, payment collection is a separate, manual step — the customer pays by check or you take their card over the phone and manually record it. With a payment processor connected, customers can pay directly on their invoice, autopay can collect recurring charges automatically, and every payment records to the ledger without any manual entry.

This session has two parts: the technical setup of connecting the processor, and the operational setup of adding payment methods to customers and configuring autopay.

→ Connects ToPayment Processors connect to: the customer's Billing tab (Payment Accounts sub-tab where cards and bank accounts are stored), Invoices (customers can pay directly from the invoice link), Autopay (processor charges the stored payment method automatically), and the Ledger (all payments record automatically). The Customer Portal also uses the payment processor to allow self-service payments.
▲ Platform ImpactWithout a connected processor, every payment requires manual intervention. For businesses with large numbers of recurring customers, this is hours of administrative work every month that could be fully automated.
2
Connecting a Processor

Go to Admin > Payment Settings and select your processor. You'll enter the API credentials provided by your payment gateway — typically an API key and merchant ID from your processor's admin portal. Once connected, PSA will confirm the connection and you can run a test transaction to verify everything works before going live.

❓ PromptDo you have your processor credentials with you today?
💡 Internal NoteDo not attempt to configure this without credentials in hand. If they don't have them today, schedule a follow-up. This is a sensitive integration step and should not be rushed.
3
Payment Accounts

Once the processor is connected, you can store payment methods on customer accounts. Go to Customer > Billing tab > Payment Accounts > Add Payment Account. Enter or tokenize a credit card or bank account (ACH). PSA stores a secure token — the actual card number never touches your system.

You can add multiple payment methods per customer and set one as the default. The default is what's used for autopay and for one-click payment processing.

❓ PromptWhich customers have a payment method on file with you today that we should add to PSA?
4
Processing Payments & Autopay

One-time payment: Customer profile > Quick Actions > Add Payment. Select the amount, method, and invoice(s) to apply it to. Processes immediately and records to the Ledger.

Autopay: Customer > Billing tab > Payment Accounts > Manage Autopay. Enable it, select the stored payment method, and set the trigger — either when the invoice is posted or when it reaches its due date. PSA charges the card automatically and records the payment.

❓ PromptWhich of your recurring customers should be on autopay? Let's set those up now.
▲ Platform ImpactAutopay is most valuable for managed service customers with predictable monthly invoices. It eliminates the need to chase payments, reduces your days sales outstanding, and gives customers a predictable, hassle-free billing experience.
