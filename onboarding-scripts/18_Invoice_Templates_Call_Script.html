Pre-Call Checklist

  • Navigate to Admin > Invoice Templates

  • Know their standard payment terms and any footer language they want
1
Why Templates Matter

Every invoice and quote that goes out to a customer is formatted by a template. The template controls your logo, your company header, the layout of line items, and the footer — where payment terms, late fee language, and contact information for billing questions live.

A well-configured template means every document that leaves your company looks professional and consistent, reinforces your brand, and includes the legal and payment language you need on every customer-facing document. It also means you configure this once and it applies automatically to every invoice and quote going forward.

→ Connects ToInvoice Templates are used whenever an invoice is generated — through individual billing, batch billing, or ticket close. Quote Templates are used when a proposal is created and sent to a customer. Both templates reference your company information from System Settings. The same system supports separate templates for different document types or brands if needed.
2
Building Your Invoice Template

Go to Admin > Invoice Templates > edit the default or create a new one.

  • Logo — upload your company logo. PNG or JPG at a reasonable resolution. Position it in the header where you want it.
  • Company Header — verify name and address match what was set in System Settings.
  • Footer — this is where your legal and payment language goes. Standard footer content includes: payment terms (Net 30, Due on Receipt), late fee language, billing contact information, and a thank-you message.
❓ PromptWhat are your standard payment terms? Do you charge late fees, and if so, what are they? What contact should customers reach for billing questions?
💡 Internal NoteIf they don't have footer language drafted, suggest starting with payment terms and a simple 'Thank you for your business' line. They can always refine it later. Don't let this slow down the session.
3
Quote Templates

Quote Templates follow the same configuration process but are used for customer-facing quotes and proposals. Go to Admin > Quote Templates. Keep the branding consistent — same logo, same header — but the footer language will differ. A quote footer typically includes the validity period ('This estimate is valid for 30 days'), acceptance instructions, and any terms specific to project commencement.

❓ PromptDo you want your quotes to carry different messaging than your invoices, or will the footer language be the same?
▲ Platform ImpactA quote without a validity period creates ambiguity — a customer can accept a quote six months later and expect the price to hold. A clear 'valid for 30 days' on the template eliminates that situation automatically.

Action Items

  • Upload your company logo
  • Finalize footer language: payment terms, late fee policy, billing contact
  • Configure a Quote Template in addition to the Invoice Template
  • Preview a test invoice to confirm everything looks correct before sending real invoices
