1
What Bill Profiles Are and Why They Exist

Bill Profiles are reusable sets of billing rules that you build once and assign to customers. Instead of configuring payment terms, invoice delivery method, billing frequency, and late fee rules on every customer individually — you build a profile for each distinct set of rules your business uses and assign the right profile to each account.

For example: you might have a 'Monthly Net 30' profile for enterprise customers, a 'Due on Receipt' profile for residential accounts, and a 'Quarterly Autopay' profile for managed service clients on autopay. Every customer on that profile gets the same billing behavior automatically.

→ Connects ToBill Profiles apply to the customer's billing behavior throughout the platform. The billing frequency in the profile influences how often invoices are generated. Payment terms flow onto invoices. Late fee rules trigger automatically when invoices pass their due date. Invoice delivery method controls how documents are sent. Autopay settings in the profile work with the connected Payment Processor.
▲ Platform ImpactWithout Bill Profiles, billing behavior is inconsistent across your customer base — some get Net 30, some get Net 15, some get emailed invoices, some don't. When customers call with billing questions, there's no single source of truth for what their terms are. Profiles make that consistent and auditable.
2
Creating a Bill Profile

Navigate to Bill Profiles > Add Profile. Key settings:

  • Profile Name — descriptive. 'Monthly Net 30,' 'Residential Due on Receipt,' 'Managed Service Autopay.'
  • Billing Frequency — how often invoices generate for customers on this profile.
  • Payment Terms — Net 15, Net 30, Net 60, Due on Receipt. This prints on every invoice for customers on this profile.
  • Invoice Delivery Method — Email, Portal, Print, or a combination.
  • Late Fee Rules — flat fee or percentage applied after a defined number of days past due. Triggers automatically.
  • Autopay — whether customers on this profile are automatically enrolled in autopay when a payment method is on file.
❓ PromptDo you use different billing rules for different types of customers? Let's identify the distinct profiles your business needs and build them.
3
Assigning Profiles to Customers

Once profiles are built, assign them from the customer record under the Billing tab. Every customer should have a Bill Profile assigned before you go live. This is what ensures PSA bills each account the right way from the first invoice.

💡 Internal NoteIf all customers follow the same billing rules, one profile is enough. Build additional profiles only where meaningful, real differences exist in how you bill different segments.

Action Items

  • Identify all distinct billing rule sets used across your customer base
  • Build a Bill Profile for each
  • Assign the correct profile to every customer account before go-live
  • Review late fee rules with finance or legal before enabling them
