1
What Accounting Periods Do and Why They Matter

Accounting Periods define the time boundaries for your financial records in Rev.io PSA — typically calendar months. Every charge, payment, credit, and invoice that gets posted belongs to the period it was posted in. When you close a period, those records are locked — nothing can be backdated into a closed period, and the financials for that period are final.

This is important for two reasons. First, it gives you clean, reliable financial reporting — your numbers for March are your numbers for March, and they won't change after the fact. Second, if you sync to an accounting system like QuickBooks or Sage, closing periods ensures the data that flows over is finalized and won't be revised.

→ Connects ToAccounting Periods affect all financial activity in the Billing module — charges, invoices, payments, credits, and adjustments all belong to an open period. Period closure is often coordinated with accounting integrations, where the period close in PSA triggers or aligns with an export to the external accounting system. Reporting by period gives finance teams the clean monthly snapshots they need for reconciliation.
▲ Platform ImpactWithout closing periods consistently, your financial reporting becomes unreliable — old transactions can be backdated, which makes month-over-month comparisons inaccurate and creates reconciliation headaches with your accounting team. A consistent period close process is foundational to financial integrity.
2
Setting Up and Understanding Periods

Navigate to Accounting Periods. PSA typically generates periods automatically based on calendar months — you'll see a list of open and closed periods. The current month is open. Charges, invoices, and payments post into the open period until you close it at the end of the month.

❓ PromptDo you use calendar months as your accounting periods, or do you operate on a custom fiscal calendar?
💡 Internal NoteMost clients use calendar months. If they use a non-standard fiscal year, note it and check with your implementation team to ensure periods are configured correctly.
3
The Period Close Process

At month-end, your process should be: review all posted charges and confirm nothing is missing, verify invoices have been sent and payments applied, then close the period. Once closed, it's locked. Any corrections that are needed after a close must be handled in the current open period — through a credit memo, adjustment, or rebill.

❓ PromptWho on your team will be responsible for closing accounting periods each month? And is there an accounting team or system we need to coordinate with on timing?

If you're using an accounting integration, coordinate your PSA period close with your export schedule. You generally want to close the period in PSA, then trigger the export so the accounting system receives finalized data.

Action Items

  • Confirm your accounting period structure — calendar month or custom
  • Identify who is responsible for the month-end close process
  • Build a month-end close checklist: charges reviewed, invoices sent, payments applied, period closed
  • If using an accounting integration, align the PSA close schedule with your export timing
